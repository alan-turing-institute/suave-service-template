Events from the year '''2010 in the United Kingdom'''.

==Incumbents==
*[[Monarch of the United Kingdom|Monarch]] – [[Elizabeth II]]
*[[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|Prime Minister]] – [[Gordon Brown]] ([[Labour Party (UK)|Labour]]) (until 11 May), [[David Cameron]] ([[Coalition Government 2010-2015|Coalition]]) (starting 11 May)

==Events==

===January===
[[File:Great Britain Snowy.jpg|thumb|280px|Satellite photo of Great Britain and Ireland during the cold spell.]]
*3 January – The prime Minister [[Gordon Brown]] announced that full [[Security scan|body scanners]] would be introduced at UK airports following the failed attack on [[Northwest Airlines Flight 253]] on 25 December 2009.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk/8438355.stm|title=Gordon Brown promises full body scanners at UK airports|publisher=BBC News|date=3 January 2010|accessdate=5 January 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100107061437/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk/8438355.stm| archivedate= 7 January 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*5 January – The country was once again deluged by heavy [[snowfall]] as it endured its worst [[Winter of 2009–2010 in the United Kingdom|cold spell]] since the winter of 1981–82.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk/8440601.stm|title=Snow and ice leads to travel delays and school closures|publisher=BBC News|date=5 January 2010|accessdate=5 January 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100114063054/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk/8440601.stm| archivedate= 14 January 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*10 January – The ''[[Sunday Mirror]]'' defence correspondent [[Rupert Hamer (journalist)|Rupert Hamer]] was killed in an explosion in Afghanistan, the [[Ministry of Defence (United Kingdom)|Ministry of Defence]] confirmed.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk/8450603.stm|title=Afghan blast kills Sunday Mirror correspondent|publisher=BBC News|date=10 January 2010|accessdate=10 January 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100114095603/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk/8450603.stm| archivedate= 14 January 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*12 January – [[Alastair Campbell]], former government advisor, was interviewed by the [[The Iraq Inquiry|Chilcot Inquiry]], and said he was prepared to defend "every word" of the September 2002 dossier on Iraq's supposed weapons of mass destruction which led to the invasion of Iraq.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/8453563.stm|title=The Full Story: Alastair Campbell faces Iraq inquiry|publisher=BBC News|date=12 January 2010|accessdate=12 January 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100114043157/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/8453563.stm| archivedate= 14 January 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*18 January – Following the collapse of strike talks late last year, [[British Airways]] cabin crew decided to vote again on possible strike action.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/8465208.stm|title=British Airways strike vote for cabin crew|publisher=BBC News|date=18 January 2010|accessdate=24 January 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100121105706/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/8465208.stm| archivedate= 21 January 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*20 January – Unemployment fell for the first time in nearly two years, with the national total for November 2009 dipping by 7,000 to 2,460,000. However, some regions of Britain were still enduring a rise in unemployment, and experts said that the slight reduction in unemployment was largely due to an increase in people taking part-time work and work in occupations largely unrelated to their skills and experience.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/8469648.stm|title=Unemployment falls to 2.46m|publisher=BBC News|date=20 January 2010|accessdate=24 January 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100123031108/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/8469648.stm| archivedate= 23 January 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*26 January – The [[Office for National Statistics]] announced that the UK was no longer in recession, with [[GDP|gross domestic product]] having grown by 0.1%, a weaker rise than many economists had expected.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/8479639.stm|title=UK economy emerges from recession|publisher=BBC News|date=26 January 2010|accessdate=26 January 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20120306222920/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/8479639.stm| archivedate=6 March 2012| deadurl= no}}</ref>
*29 January – former Prime Minister [[Tony Blair]] appeared at the [[Iraq Inquiry]] and was questioned in public for the first time about his decision to take the United Kingdom to [[Iraq War|war]] against [[Iraq]].<ref>{{cite news|url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/uk_news/politics/8485694.stm|title= Iraq inquiry hears defiant Blair say: I'd do it again|publisher= BBC News|date= 29 January 2010|accessdate= 6 February 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100206190613/http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/uk_news/politics/8485694.stm| archivedate= 6 February 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.theguardian.com/uk/2010/jan/29/tony-blair-iraq-war-inquiry|title= Iraq war inquiry: Tony Blair arrives early to avoid protesters|publisher= ''The Guardian''|first= Andrew|last= Sparrow|date= 29 January 2010|accessdate= 6 February 2010 | location=London| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100203020756/http://www.guardian.co.uk/uk/2010/jan/29/tony-blair-iraq-war-inquiry| archivedate= 3 February 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>

===February===
*2 February – The [[Birmingham]] based confectionery giant [[Cadbury plc|Cadbury]] was taken over by American rival [[Kraft Foods]] in an £11.5 billion deal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/8492572.stm |title=Kraft Foods completes Cadbury takeover |publisher=BBC News |date=2 February 2010 |accessdate=27 March 2012}}</ref>
*3 February – Opinion polls indicated that [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour]] had reduced the [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative]] lead to as little as seven points, increasing the possibility of a hung parliament after the forthcoming general election.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://uk.reuters.com/article/idUKTRE6123CT20100203|title=Brown takes aim at Tories as polls narrow|publisher=Reuters|date=3 February 2010|accessdate=6 February 2010}}</ref>
*5 February – After a long period of negotiations, the political parties of [[Northern Ireland]], including the [[Democratic Unionist Party]] and [[Sinn Féin]], reached an agreement to allow for the [[Northern Ireland devolved administration|devolution]] of policing and justice powers.<ref name=NIPolicing>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/northern_ireland/8500172.stm|title=Policing and justice plan: Key points |publisher=BBC News|date=5 February 2010|accessdate=6 February 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100211184143/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/northern_ireland/8500172.stm| archivedate= 11 February 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>

===March===
*2 March
**[[British Broadcasting Corporation|BBC]] Director General [[Mark Thompson (television executive)|Mark Thompson]] confirmed proposals to close [[BBC 6 Music]] and the [[BBC Asian Network]] as part of a cost-cutting drive. The plan would also see [[BBC Radio 7]] rebranded as ''BBC Radio 4 Extra'' and cutbacks to the [[BBC Online|BBC website]].<ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.theguardian.com/media/2010/mar/02/bbc-6-music-asian-network|title= BBC confirms plans to axe 6 Music and Asian Network|publisher= The Guardian|date= 2 March 2010|accessdate= 2 March 2010|first= John|last= Plunkett | location=London| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100408145035/http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/2010/mar/02/bbc-6-music-asian-network?| archivedate= 8 April 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
**Jon Venables, one of the two boys (then aged 11) found guilty of murdering [[Merseyside]] toddler [[Murder of James Bulger|James Bulger]] in 1993, was recalled to prison after breaching terms of his [[Life imprisonment|life licence]]. Venables, at the time, 28, spent eight years in custody before being paroled along with Robert Thompson in 2001.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/merseyside/8546528.stm|title= James Bulger murderer Jon Venables returned to prison|publisher= BBC News|date= 2 March 2010|accessdate= 8 March 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100309132414/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/merseyside/8546528.stm| archivedate= 9 March 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*5 March – The prime Minister [[Gordon Brown]] gave evidence to the [[Iraq inquiry|Chilcott inquiry]].<ref>{{cite news|url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/8550779.stm|title= Iraq inquiry: Gordon Brown says war was 'right'|publisher= BBC News|date= 5 March 2010|accessdate= 5 March 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100306022441/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/8550779.stm| archivedate= 6 March 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*8 March
**[[Jack Straw]], the [[Secretary of State for Justice|Justice Secretary]], rejected ongoing public calls to give reasons why Jon Venables has been recalled to custody to be made public.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/merseyside/8555977.stm|title= Bulger killer Jon Venables' recall reasons stay secret|publisher= BBC News|date= 8 March 2010|accessdate= 8 March 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100310155357/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/merseyside/8555977.stm| archivedate= 10 March 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref> Prime Minister [[Gordon Brown]] has also reportedly refused to inform James's mother Denise Fergus of the reason.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.dailymail.co.uk/debate/article-1256078/Jon-Venables-serving-life-hes-jail-not.html|title= Jon Venables is serving life, whether he's in jail or not|publisher= Mail Online|first= Suzanne|last= Moore|date= 8 March 2010|accessdate= 8 March 2010 | location=London| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100406193511/http://www.dailymail.co.uk/debate/article-1256078/Jon-Venables-serving-life-hes-jail-not.html| archivedate= 6 April 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
**Following lengthy discussions between [[Royal Mail]] managers and [[Communication Workers Union (UK)|Communication Workers Union]] representatives agreed a deal to settle the [[2009 Royal Mail industrial disputes|postal workers dispute]].<ref name=BBC08Mar2010>{{cite news|url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/8556487.stm|title= Royal Mail union agrees pay and modernisation deal|publisher= BBC News|date= 8 March 2010|accessdate= 9 March 2010}}</ref>
*10 March – [[Chester City F.C.]], bottom of the [[Conference National|Blue Square Premier League]], go out of business after 125 years, less than a year after being relegated from the [[Football League]] where they had spent all but four seasons since 1931.<ref>{{cite web|author=Gordon Farquhar |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/gordonfarquhar/2010/03/chester_city_gone_in_30_second.html |title=Gordon Farquhar: Chester City gone in 30 seconds |publisher=BBC |date= |accessdate=27 March 2012}}</ref>
*12 March
**[[Birmingham]] couple Angela Gordon and Junaid Abuhamza received prison sentences after being convicted of the manslaughter of Ms Gordon's seven-year-old daughter Khyra Ishaq, who died as a result of starvation two years ago. Ms Gordon was sentenced to 15 years in prison, while Mr Abuhamza was sentenced to indefinite imprisonment with a recommended minimum term of seven and a half years.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/west_midlands/8551118.stm|title= Birmingham couple jailed for starving girl Khyra Ishaq|publisher= BBC News|date= 12 March 2010|accessdate= 12 March 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100314133835/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/west_midlands/8551118.stm| archivedate= 14 March 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
**The [[Unite (trade union)|Unite]] union which represents [[British Airways]] cabin crew announced two rounds of strike action for three days from 20 March and four days from 27 March.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/8563728.stm|title= BA strike: Union announces dates in March|publisher= BBC News|date= 12 March 2010|accessdate= 12 March 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100313055005/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/8563728.stm| archivedate= 13 March 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*20 March – The first British Airways strike, set to last for three days, began. More than 80 planes were grounded at [[Heathrow Airport]] alone and numerous flights were reported to have been cancelled, though British Airways officials are confident that 65% of flights will be undisturbed.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/8577573.stm|title= British Airways cabin crew strike takes effect|publisher= BBC News|date= 20 March 2010|accessdate= 20 March 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100323092240/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/8577573.stm| archivedate= 23 March 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*30 March – [[Levi Bellfield]], a 41-year-old man two years into a [[Life imprisonment|life sentence]] for murdering two women and attempting to murder a third, was charged with the murder of [[Surrey]] teenager [[Milly Dowler]], who disappeared in [[Walton-on-Thames]] eight years ago and whose body was found in [[Hampshire]] woodland six months later.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/8593861.stm |title= Man faces court over kidnap and murder of Milly Dowler  |publisher= BBC News|date= 30 March 2010|accessdate= 12 April 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100414063230/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/8593861.stm| archivedate= 14 April 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>

===April===
[[File:Eyjafjallajokull volcano plume 2010 04 18.JPG|thumb|280px|The volcanic eruption that plighted many British travellers.]]
*6 April –  The prime minister, [[Gordon Brown]], visited [[Buckingham Palace]] to seek [[Elizabeth II|The Queen]]'s permission to dissolve Parliament on 12 April, triggering a general election on 6 May.<ref name=BBC06Apr0210>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/election_2010/8603591.stm|title=Gordon Brown calls 6 May general election|publisher=British Broadcasting Corporation|work=BBC News|date=6 April 2010|accessdate=6 April 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100408014038/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/election_2010/8603591.stm| archivedate= 8 April 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*9–11 April – [[Metal detector]]ist Dave Crisp discovered the [[Frome Hoard]], 52,503 [[Roman coin]]s dating to the period 253–305, one of the largest such finds in Britain.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://finds.org.uk/blogs/fromehoard/|title=The Frome Hoard|publisher=[[Portable Antiquities Scheme]]|accessdate=8 July 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100712200444/http://finds.org.uk/blogs/fromehoard/| archivedate= 12 July 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*12 April
**Policing and justice powers were [[Northern Ireland devolved administration|devolved]] from [[Government of the United Kingdom|Westminster]] to the [[Northern Ireland Executive]]. As part of the devolution process, [[David Ford]], the leader of the [[Alliance Party of Northern Ireland]], was elected [[Minister of Justice (Northern Ireland)|Minister of Justice]] by the [[Northern Ireland Assembly]].<ref>{{cite news|url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/northern_ireland/8615741.stm|title=David Ford secures justice job|publisher=BBC News|date=12 April 2010|accessdate=12 April 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100415064657/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/northern_ireland/8615741.stm| archivedate= 15 April 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
**Minutes later, a car bomb exploded outside the [[MI5]] headquarters in [[County Down]], Northern Ireland. The [[Real IRA]] claimed responsibility for the bomb shortly after its detonation.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/northern_ireland/8614723.stm|title=Real IRA admits Northern Ireland MI5 base car bomb|publisher=BBC News|date=12 April 2010|accessdate=12 April 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100412054123/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/northern_ireland/8614723.stm| archivedate= 12 April 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*15 April
**A cloud of [[volcanic ash]] from the [[2010 eruption of Eyjafjallajökull|eruption]] of [[Eyjafjallajökull]] in Iceland caused the [[Air travel disruption after the 2010 Eyjafjallajökull eruption|closure of airspace]] over the United Kingdom and northern and western Europe.<ref>{{cite news|url= https://www.theguardian.com/travel/2010/apr/15/iceland-volcano-flights-disruption|title= UK flights banned as volcanic ash causes disruption across Europe|publisher= Guardian Media Group|work= The Guardian|first= James|last= Meikle|date= 15 April 2010|accessdate= 15 April 2010 | location=London| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100417141748/http://www.guardian.co.uk/travel/2010/apr/15/iceland-volcano-flights-disruption| archivedate= 17 April 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
**[[ITV1]] aired the first of three [[United Kingdom general election debates, 2010|election debates]] between [[Gordon Brown]], [[David Cameron]] and [[Nick Clegg]] ahead of the [[United Kingdom general election, 2010|2010 general election]].<ref>{{cite news|url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/election_2010/8621119.stm|title= Election 2010: Three way clashes in historic TV debate|publisher= British Broadcasting Corporation|work= BBC News|date= 15 April 2010|accessdate= 15 April 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100415033822/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/election_2010/8621119.stm| archivedate= 15 April 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*21 April – The government announced that British airports would reopen and passenger flights would resume, but officials cautioned that it would take time for flight schedules to return to normal after the [[Air travel disruption after the 2010 Eyjafjallajökull eruption|six-day shutdown]] caused by volcanic ash from the [[2010 eruptions of Eyjafjallajökull|2010 eruptions]] of the [[Eyjafjallajökull]] volcano.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://content.usatoday.com/communities/ondeadline/post/2010/04/british-airports-set-to-reopen-/1|title= British airports reopen; returning stranded fliers could take weeks|work= USA Today|date= 20 April 2010|accessdate= 7 May 2010|first=Michael|last=Winter}}</ref>

*6 May
**The [[United Kingdom general election, 2010|2010 general election]] took place, resulting in a [[hung parliament]]. The [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party]] won a plurality of seats, 306 of the 649 contested, placing them 20 seats short of an overall majority in the [[British House of Commons|House of Commons]], whilst the [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] won 258 seats, and the [[Liberal Democrats (UK)|Liberal Democrats]] 57.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/shared/election2010/results/ |title=BBC News – Election 2010 – Results | publisher=BBC News |date=7 May 2010 | accessdate=9 May 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100414102452/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/shared/election2010/results/| archivedate=14 April 2010| deadurl= no}}</ref>
**[[Caroline Lucas]], leader of the [[Green Party of England and Wales]], became the party's first Westminster MP, being elected in [[Brighton Pavilion (UK Parliament constituency)|Brighton Pavilion]],<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/shared/election2010/results/constituency/a69.stm |title=BBC News – Election 2010 – Constituency – Brighton Pavilion | publisher=BBC News |date=7 May 2010 | accessdate=9 May 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100523081055/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/shared/election2010/results/constituency/a69.stm| archivedate= 23 May 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref> and [[Peter Robinson (Northern Ireland politician)|Peter Robinson]], [[First Minister of Northern Ireland]] and [[Democratic Unionist Party]] leader, unexpectedly lost his [[Belfast East (UK Parliament constituency)|Belfast East]] seat to the [[Alliance Party of Northern Ireland|Alliance Party]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/shared/election2010/results/constituency/701.stm |title=BBC News – Election 2010 – Constituency – Belfast East | publisher=BBC News |date=7 May 2010 | accessdate=9 May 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100427010724/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/shared/election2010/results/constituency/701.stm| archivedate= 27 April 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
**[[United Kingdom local elections, 2010|Local elections]] were also held across England in all 32 [[London Boroughs]], all 36 [[Metropolitan Borough]]s, 20 [[Unitary Authorities]] and 76 [[Non-metropolitan district]]s. The Labour Party gained 15 councils to control 36 overall, the Conservatives suffered a net loss of 8 councils, leaving them in control of 65, and the Liberal Democrats suffered a net loss of 4 local authorities, being left in control of 13 councils.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/nol/shared/election2010/council/html/region_99999.stm |title=BBC News – Election 2010 – Local Councils | publisher=BBC News |date=7 May 2010 | accessdate=9 May 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100510030148/http://news.bbc.co.uk/nol/shared/election2010/council/html/region_99999.stm| archivedate= 10 May 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*7 May – The [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party]] and the [[Liberal Democrats (UK)|Liberal Democrats]] began [[2010 United Kingdom government formation|negotiations]] to reach either a parliamentary agreement, or to create a [[coalition government]] with a [[British House of Commons|House of Commons]] majority.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/election_2010/8667938.stm |title=Election: Cameron makes offer to Lib Dems on government  | publisher=BBC News |date=7 May 2010 | accessdate=9 May 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20120313133152/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/election_2010/8667938.stm| archivedate=13 March 2012| deadurl= no}}</ref>
*8 May – Liberal Democrat MPs endorsed Nick Clegg's decision to negotiate with the Conservative Party in the first instance following the inconclusive result of the general election.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/election_2010/8669508.stm |title=Lib Dem leadership 'endorses' Clegg's Tory talks | publisher=BBC News |date=8 May 2010 | accessdate=9 May 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100511155626/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/election_2010/8669508.stm| archivedate= 11 May 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*9 May – [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] became champions of the [[Premier League]] after beating [[Wigan Athletic F.C.|Wigan Athletic]] 8-0 on the final day of the season.
*10 May – [[Gordon Brown]] announced that he would be stepping down as Labour leader, thus triggering a [[Labour Party (UK) leadership election, 2010|leadership election]]. Talks between the Lib Dems and Conservatives continued.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/election_2010/8672859.stm|title= Gordon Brown 'stepping down as Labour leader'|publisher= BBC|work= BBC News|date= 10 May 2010|accessdate= 10 May 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20100513074419/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/election_2010/8672859.stm| archivedate= 13 May 2010 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*11 May
**After the Labour Party failed to reach an agreement with the Liberal Democrats, Gordon Brown went to [[Buckingham Palace]] and tendered his resignation as Prime Minister to [[Queen Elizabeth II]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/election_2010/8675913.stm|title=Gordon Brown resigns as UK prime minister|publisher=BBC|work= BBC News|date=11 May 2010|accessdate=12 May 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20130601102646/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/election_2010/8675913.stm| archivedate=1 June 2013| deadurl= no}}</ref>
**With a [[Conservative – Liberal Democrat Coalition Agreement|coalition government]] between the Conservatives and Liberal Democrats still being finalised, David Cameron was appointed Prime Minister by the Queen following Brown's resignation.<ref name="NewEra">{{cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/election_2010/8676607.stm|title= David Cameron hails 'new era' as coalition sets to work|publisher= BBC|work= BBC News|date= 12 May 2010|accessdate= 12 May 2010| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20140508164759/http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/election_2010/8676607.stm| archivedate=8 May 2014| deadurl= no}}</ref>
