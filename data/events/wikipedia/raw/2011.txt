==Events==

===January===
*1 January – Inmates rioted at [[Ford Open Prison]] near [[Arundel]], West Sussex. Windows were smashed and part of the prison was set on fire by prisoners, whose rioting was believed to have been sparked by staff attempts to breathalyse prisoners, amid allegations that alcohol had been smuggled into the prison.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-12102266|title=Ford open prison riot tackled by specialist officers|publisher=[[BBC News]]|date=1 January 2011|accessdate=2 January 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110102080440/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-12102266| archivedate= 2 January 2011 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*2 January – The [[Montenegrin Embassy]] in London wrote to Scottish Labour leader [[Iain Gray]], correcting factual inaccuracies and asking him to explain comments he made during [[First Minister's Questions]] in December 2010, about the country being involved in "war crimes", "ethnic cleansing" and "a United Nations peace-keeping mission"<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dailyexpress.co.uk/posts/view/220688/Montenegro-fury-over-Gray-s-claims-of-ethnic-cleansing/|title=Montenegro fury over Gray's claims of ethnic cleansing|date=2 January 2011|work=Sunday Express|accessdate=3 January 2011|location=London}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.heraldscotland.com:80/news/politics/diplomatic-fury-after-labour-leader-s-ethnic-cleansing-jibe-1.1077459|title=Diplomatic fury after Labour leader's ethnic cleansing jibe|last=Hutcheon|first=Paul|date=2 January 2011|work=Sunday Herald|accessdate=3 January 2011|location=Glasgow}}</ref>
*4 January – [[Value added tax]] increased to 20% from 17.5%.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-12099638|title=VAT rises from 17.5% to 20%|date=4 January 2010|publisher=BBC News |accessdate=4 January 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110104050234/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-12099638| archivedate= 4 January 2011 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*5 January – Music retailer [[HMV]] announced the closure of 60 stores following disappointing Christmas sales – a move which would see the firm lose 10% of its stores and could cost up to 900 people their jobs.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-12117510|title= HMV to close 60 stores as sales and shares slump|publisher=BBC News |date= 5 January 2011|accessdate= 23 January 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110120064351/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-12117510| archivedate= 20 January 2011 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*7 January –
** The [[England cricket team]] won [[The Ashes]] series 3–1 in Australia.
** Former Labour Member of Parliament [[David Chaytor]] was jailed for 18 months for fraudulently claiming more than £20,000 in expenses.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-12127327|title=MPs' expenses: David Chaytor jailed over false claims|date=7 January 2010|publisher=BBC News |accessdate=7 January 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110107050637/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-12127327| archivedate= 7 January 2011 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
** The film ''[[The King's Speech]]'', with [[Colin Firth]] as the [[stuttering]] [[George VI]] was released in the UK. It won four [[Academy Award]]s, including [[Academy Award for Best Picture|Best Picture]], on 27 February.
* 9 January – An investigation by ''[[The Guardian]]'' newspaper revealed details of how Metropolitan police officer [[Mark Kennedy (police officer)|Mark Kennedy]] infiltrated dozens of protest groups in 22 countries using the pseudonym Mark Stone.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/uk/2011/jan/09/undercover-office-green-activists|title=Undercover officer spied on green activists|work=The Guardian|date= 9 January 2011|accessdate=31 December 2011}}</ref>
*13 January – [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour]] win the [[Oldham East and Saddleworth by-election, 2011|Oldham East and Saddleworth by-election]].
*15 January – Three former [[Church of England]] bishops were [[ordained]] as [[Priesthood (Catholic Church)|priests]] in the Roman Catholic [[Personal Ordinariate of Our Lady of Walsingham]] at [[Westminster Cathedral]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-12197985|title=Ex-Anglican bishops ordained as Catholics|date=15 January 2010|publisher=BBC News |accessdate=15 January 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110115052746/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-12197985| archivedate= 15 January 2011 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*18 January – A gay couple won a discrimination case against Christian hoteliers who refused to let them stay in a double room.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2011/jan/18/gay-couple-win-case-hoteliers|title=Gay couple wins discrimination case against Christian hoteliers|work=The Guardian|date=18 January 2011|accessdate=31 December 2011|author=Owen Bowcott}}</ref>
*21 January
** [[News International phone hacking scandal|''News of the World'' phone hacking affair]]: [[Andy Coulson]], the former editor of the ''[[News of the World]]'', resigned from his position as [[David Cameron]]'s communications director, citing "continued coverage of events connected to my old job at the ''News of the World''".<ref name="resignationcoulson">{{Cite news|title=Coulson resigns due to phone-hacking scandal|work=The Spy Report|publisher=Media Spy|date=21 January 2011|url=http://www.mediaspy.org/report/2011/01/21/uk-coulson-resigns-due-to-phone-hacking-scandal/ |accessdate=26 January 2011}}</ref>
** [[Tony Blair]] appeared before the [[Iraq Inquiry|Chilcot Inquiry]] into the [[Iraq War]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2011/jan/21/chilcot-inquiry-blair-jonathan-freedland|title=The Chilcot inquiry's moment of astonishing emotional intensity|work=The Guardian|date=21 January 2011|accessdate=31 December 2011}}</ref>
** [[Alan Johnson]] resigned as shadow chancellor, he was succeeded by [[Ed Balls]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2011/jan/20/alan-johnson-quits-labour-balls|title=Alan Johnson resigns as shadow chancellor|work=The Guardian|date=21 January 2011|accessdate=31 December 2011}}</ref>
*25 January
** Statistics revealed that the UK economy contracted by 0.5% during the final quarter of last year.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-12272717|title= UK economy suffers 0.5% contraction|publisher=BBC News |date=25 January 2011|accessdate=27 January 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110126152135/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-12272717| archivedate= 26 January 2011 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
** [[Sky Sports]] presenter [[Andy Gray (footballer born 1955)|Andy Gray]] was sacked for sexist comments made about a female football official.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/football/2011/jan/25/andy-gray-sacked-sky|title=Andy Gray sacked by Sky for 'unacceptable and offensive behaviour'|work=The Guardian|date=25 January 2011|accessdate=31 December 2011}}</ref>
*26 January – Prime Minister [[David Cameron]] announced that [[Sinn Féin]]'s [[Gerry Adams]] had [[Resignation from the British House of Commons|resigned from the British parliament]] and had accepted the position of [[Crown Steward and Bailiff of the Manor of Northstead]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.hm-treasury.gov.uk/press_08_11.htm|title=British Treasury|date=26 January 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.independent.ie/national-news/elections/adams-resigns-from-westminister-seat-2512403.html |archive-url=https://archive.is/20120804150049/http://www.independent.ie/national-news/elections/adams-resigns-from-westminister-seat-2512403.html |dead-url=yes |archive-date= 4 August 2012 |title=Adams resigns from Westminister seat |work=Irish Independent |date=26 January 2011 |df= }}</ref> Speaker [[John Bercow]] later clarifies that Adams has been appointed to the role following a denial of his acceptance.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-12294406|title=John Bercow says Gerry Adams is not an MP|publisher=BBC News|date=26 January 2011|accessdate=27 January 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110127092833/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-12294406| archivedate=27 January 2011|deadurl=no}}</ref>

* 3 March – Voters in [[Wales]] approved plans to give the [[Welsh Assembly]] more powers.
* 3 March – The Labour Party won the [[Barnsley Central by-election, 2011|Barnsley Central By-election]], with the [[Liberal Democrats (UK)|Liberal Democrats]] finishing in sixth place.
* 9 March – The [[Serious Fraud Office (United Kingdom)|Serious Fraud Office]] arrested [[Robert Tchenguiz|Robert]] and [[Vincent Tchenguiz]] in connection with the collapse of the Icelandic [[Kaupthing Bank]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/business/2011/mar/09/tchenguiz-brothers-arrested-kaupthing-collapse-iceland|work=The Guardian|date=10 March 2011|accessdate=31 December 2011|title=Tchenguiz brothers arrested in Kaupthing investigation}}</ref>
* 11 March – [[Light aircraft carrier]] [[HMS Ark Royal (R07)|HMS ''Ark Royal'' (1981)]], [[flagship]] of the [[Royal Navy]], was [[Ship decommissioning|decommissioned]], as part of the naval restructuring portion of the 2010 [[Strategic Defence and Security Review 2010|Strategic Defence and Security Review]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Final farewell for decommissioned warship HMS Ark Royal|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-hampshire-12706441|publisher=[[BBC News]]|date=11 March 2011|accessdate=11 March 2011}}</ref>
* 18 March – Former [[British Airways]] software engineer Rajib Karim, of [[Newcastle upon Tyne]] was jailed for 30 years after he was earlier convicted of plotting to blow up a plane.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-12788224|title= Terror plot BA man Rajib Karim gets 30 years|publisher=BBC News |date= 18 March 2011|accessdate= 20 March 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110318231900/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-12788224| archivedate= 18 March 2011 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
* 19 March – [[Operation Unified Protector]]: British, French and American military initiated air strikes in [[Libya]] following [[United Nations Security Council Resolution 1973]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2011/mar/19/libya-air-strikes-gaddafi-france|title=Allied strikes sweep Libya as west intervenes in conflict|work=The Guardian|date=20 March 2011|accessdate=31 December 2011}}</ref>
* 26 March – Hundreds of thousands of people marched in London against [[Government of the United Kingdom|government]] budget cuts with the protests later turning violent.<ref>[http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/politics/8407260/TUC-protest-march-live.html (''The Telegraph'')] [https://www.theguardian.com/society/blog/2011/mar/26/march-for-the-alternative-live-blog-updates?CMP=twt_gu (''The Guardian'')]</ref>

===April===
*1 April – ''[[The Daily Sport]]'' and ''[[Sunday Sport]]'' [[Tabloid (newspaper format)|tabloid]] newspapers ceased publication and enter administration.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pressgazette.co.uk/story.asp?sectioncode=1&storycode=46915&c=1|first=Paul|last=McNally|title=Daily Sport ceases publication and calls in administrators|work=Press Gazette|date=1 April 2011|accessdate=4 April 2011}}</ref>
*3 April – The UK's last [[circus]] elephant retired.<ref>{{cite news|first=Steven|last=Morris|title=Anne says goodbye to the circus|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2011/apr/07/anne-the-elephant-goodbye-circus|work=The Guardian|date=7 April 2011|accessdate=7 April 2011|page=3| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110413064732/http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2011/apr/07/anne-the-elephant-goodbye-circus| archivedate= 13 April 2011 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*4 April – As part of the British government's package of welfare reforms, starting from this date the one-and-a-half million people in the United Kingdom who were claiming [[incapacity benefit]] began to receive letters asking them to attend a work capability assessment. The tests are part of government plans to reduce the number of long-term claimants and will take until 2014 to complete.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-12954116|title=Incapacity benefit crackdown begins after pilot scheme|publisher=BBC News |date=4 April 2011|accessdate=5 April 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110405193507/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-12954116| archivedate= 5 April 2011 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*5 April – Police investigating the [[murder of Sian O'Callaghan]] identified human remains found at a second site as those of [[Swindon]] woman Becky Godden-Edwards,<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-12970112|title=Sian O'Callaghan police identify body found in Eastleach|publisher=BBC News |date=5 April 2011|accessdate=5 April 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110405190812/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-12970112| archivedate= 5 April 2011 | deadurl= no}}</ref> who was last seen alive in 2002 at the age of 20.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/debate/article-1374689/Sian-OCallaghan-murder-Rebecca-Godden-Edwards-lost-soul-cut-adrift-drugs.html|title= Rebecca, just one more lost soul cut adrift by drugs|first=Jan|last=Moir|work=Daily Mail|date=8 April 2011|accessdate=9 April 2011}}</ref>
*6 April – The [[Mandatory retirement]] age began to be phased out,<ref>{{cite web|first=Clive|last=Coleman|title=Default retirement age scrapped allowing people to work beyond 65|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-12982824|publisher=BBC News |date=6 April 2011|accessdate=7 April 2011}}</ref> being fully abolished by 1 October.<ref>{{cite web|title=Compulsory retirement age at 65 fully abolished|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-15127835|publisher=BBC News |date=1 October 2011|accessdate=1 October 2011}}</ref>
*13 April – 53-year-old actor [[Brian Regan (actor)|Brian Regan]], most famous for his role as [[Terry Sullivan (Brookside)|Terry Sullivan]] in the former [[Channel 4]] TV soap ''[[Brookside]]'', was charged – along with another man – with the murder of a man who was fatally shot in [[Aigburth]], Merseyside, on 24 February 2011.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-merseyside-13059167|title=Former Brookside star Brian Regan charged with murder|publisher=BBC News |date=13 April 2011|accessdate=16 April 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110415091349/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-merseyside-13059167| archivedate= 15 April 2011 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*24 April – Senior Liberal Democrat minister [[Chris Huhne]] threatened legal action over "untruths" told by Conservative MP's opposed to the Alternative Vote System, 11 days before the referendum. He also warned that the dispute could damage the coalition government.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-13181756|title= AV campaign rows causing coalition conflict, says Huhne|publisher=BBC News |date=24 April 2011|accessdate=28 April 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110425042146/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-13181756| archivedate= 25 April 2011 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*27 April – The Office for National Statistics revealed that the economy had returned to growth during the first quarter of this year, growing by 0.5%.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-13206430|title=UK economy grows by 0.5% in first quarter of 2011|publisher=BBC News |date=27 April 2011|accessdate=28 April 2011| archiveurl= https://web.archive.org/web/20110428042103/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-13206430| archivedate= 28 April 2011 | deadurl= no}}</ref>
*29 April – [[Prince William, Duke of Cambridge]] and [[Catherine, Duchess of Cambridge|Catherine Middleton]] [[Wedding of Prince William, Duke of Cambridge, and Catherine Middleton|married]] in [[Westminster Abbey]]. A public holiday celebrated the day, which in conjunction with the May Bank Holiday, made a four-day weekend.
