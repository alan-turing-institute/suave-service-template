Events from the year '''2012 in the United Kingdom'''.
This year was the [[Diamond Jubilee of Elizabeth II]].

==Incumbents==
*[[Monarch of the United Kingdom|Monarch]] – [[Elizabeth II]] 
*[[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|Prime Minister]] – [[David Cameron]] ([[Coalition Government 2010-2015|Coalition]])

==Events==

===January===
*3 January – After a trial based on new [[forensic evidence]], Gary Dobson and David Norris are convicted of the racist murder of black London teenager [[Stephen Lawrence]], who was killed in April 1993.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-16347953|title=Stephen Lawrence murder: Dobson and Norris guilty|publisher=BBC|work=[[BBC News]]|date=3 January 2012|accessdate=3 January 2012}}</ref>
*4 January – Gary Dobson and David Norris are [[life imprisonment|jailed for life]] for the murder of Stephen Lawrence, with minimum term of just over 15 and 14 years respectively.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-16403655 |title=BBC News – Stephen Lawrence: Gary Dobson and David Norris get life |publisher=Bbc.co.uk |date=4 January 2012 |accessdate=4 January 2012}}</ref>
*6 January – Mobile operator [[O2 (UK)|O2]] announces plans to provide free internet to millions of residents and visitors in central London, by launching Europe's largest free Wi-Fi zone.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/technology-16440911|title='Europe's biggest' free wi-fi zone set for London|publisher=BBC|work=[[BBC News]]|date=6 January 2012|accessdate=6 January 2012}}</ref>
*10 January
** The [[Scottish Government]] announces that it plans to hold the [[Independence referendum, Scotland|referendum on Scottish independence]] in the autumn of 2014.<ref name="BBC">{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-16478121|title=Scotland to hold independence poll in 2014 – Salmond|date=10 January 2012|publisher=BBC|accessdate=10 January 2012}}</ref>
** Five Muslim men go on trial at [[Derby]] Crown Court for calling for gay men to be killed, the first such prosecution under hate crime legislation.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/uk/2012/jan/10/muslim-anti-gay-leaflet-hate|title=Muslim men accused of hate crime over anti-gay leaflet|work=[[The Guardian]]|publisher=Guardian Media Group|date=10 January 2012|accessdate=11 January 2012}}</ref>
*20 January – [[Press TV]], an English language news channel owned by the [[Government of Iran|Iranian Government]] is forced off air in the United Kingdom after [[Ofcom]] revokes its broadcasting licence for breaching the terms of the [[Communications Act 2003|Communications Act]].<ref>{{cite news|first=Mark|last=Sweney|url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2012/jan/20/iran-press-tv-loses-uk-licence?newsfeed=true|title=Iran's Press TV loses UK licence|work=The Guardian|date=20 January 2012|accessdate=4 February 2012}}</ref>
*21 January – Under new guidelines to come into force from 30 April clinics which charge for pregnancy services including [[abortion]]s will be able to advertise on radio and television after the [[Broadcast Committee of Advertising Practice]] ruled there was no justification for barring such clinics from advertising their services.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-16663800|title= Abortion clinics cleared for TV by advertising body|publisher=BBC|work=BBC News|date=21 January 2012|accessdate=21 January 2012}}</ref>
*23 January – John Anslow, a prisoner charged with murder following a fatal shooting in 2010, escapes from the van transporting him to a court appearance following an armed ambush near [[Redditch]], [[Worcestershire]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-hereford-worcester-16682569 |work=BBC News |title=Prisoner escapes after van ambush in Worcestershire |publisher=BBC |date=23 January 2012 |accessdate=24 January 2012}}</ref>
*24 January – UK government debt has risen above £1,000,000,000,000 for the first time.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-16698293 |work=BBC News |title=Government debt rises to a record of £1tn |publisher=BBC |date=24 January 2012 |accessdate=24 January 2012}}</ref>
*25 January
**Scotland's First Minister, [[Alex Salmond]], sets out the question – "Do you agree that Scotland should be an independent country?" – that he intends to ask voters in a referendum in 2014.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-16702392 |work=BBC News |title=Scots referendum question set out |publisher=BBC |date=25 January 2012 |accessdate=25 January 2012}}</ref>
**Official figures reveal that the UK economy shrunk by 0.2% in the final three months of 2011.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-16715080 |work=BBC News |title=UK economy shrinks by 0.2% in last three months of 2011 |publisher=BBC |date=25 January 2012 |accessdate=25 January 2012}}</ref>
*31 January – Former [[Royal Bank of Scotland]] CEO [[Fred Goodwin]] loses his [[knighthood]] as a result of the near collapse of the bank in 2008.<ref>{{cite news|author = |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-16821650 |work=BBC News |title=Former RBS boss Fred Goodwin stripped of knighthood |publisher=BBC |date=31 January 2012 |accessdate=1 February 2012}}</ref>

===February===
*February – [[Ash dieback]] fungus first found in the British Isles.
*3 February –
**[[Secretary of State for Energy and Climate Change|Secretary of State for Energy]] [[Chris Huhne]] resigns after the [[Crown Prosecution Service]] announces it will bring [[R v Huhne and Pryce|charges against him]] over claims his wife accepted penalty points on her [[Driving licence in the United Kingdom|driving licence]] for speeding on his behalf.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-16866127|work=BBC News|title=Chris Huhne quits cabinet over speeding claims charge|publisher=BBC|date=3 February 2012|accessdate=3 February 2012}}</ref>
**The [[Football Association]] removes [[John Terry]] as Captain of the [[England national football team]] over allegations of racial abuse of opponent [[Anton Ferdinand]] during a match.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/16866149|work=BBC Sport |title=John Terry: FA strips England captaincy from Chelsea defender|publisher=BBC|date=3 February 2012|accessdate=5 February 2012}}</ref>
*4 February – The [[Met Office]] issues a [[severe weather warning]] as [[Early 2012 European cold wave|heavy snow falls across much of the UK]], disrupting road and air travel.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-16883674|work=BBC News|title=Heavy snow falling across much of UK|publisher=BBC|date=4 February 2012|accessdate=4 February 2012}}</ref>
*6 February – [[Elizabeth II|Queen Elizabeth II]] celebrates [[Diamond Jubilee of Elizabeth II|her Diamond Jubilee]], marking sixty years on the throne; only the second British monarch to do so.<ref>{{cite news|author =Peter Hunt |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-16896731 |title=BBC News – Diamond Jubilee: Queen celebrating 60-year reign |publisher=Bbc.co.uk |date=6 February 2012 |accessdate=2012-02-10}}</ref>
*9 February – The Bank of England agrees to extend its quantitative easing programme by £50,000,000,000, to give a further boost to the UK economy.<ref>{{cite news|first=Simon |last=Gompertz |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-16963116 |work=BBC News |title=Bank of England injects another £50bn into UK economy |publisher=BBC |date=9 February 2012 |accessdate=10 February 2012}}</ref>
*17 February – [[Rupert Murdoch]] announces that a Sunday edition of ''[[The Sun (UK newspaper)|The Sun]]'' newspaper, ''The Sun on Sunday'', will be launched "very soon", effectively replacing the [[News of the World]] which was axed last summer due to the phone hacking scandal.<ref>{{cite news|author =Torin Douglas |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-17070555 |work=BBC News |title=Rupert Murdoch to launch Sun on Sunday newspaper 'soon' |publisher=BBC |date=17 February 2012 |accessdate=28 February 2012}}</ref> Its launch is confirmed on 19 February for the following weekend.
*24 February – [[Falkirk]] MP [[Eric Joyce]] is charged with three counts of common assault after a disturbance at a House of Commons bar.<ref>{{cite news|author = |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-17150381 |work=BBC Sport |title=MP Eric Joyce charged with assault |publisher=BBC |date=24 February 2012 |accessdate=24 February 2012}}</ref>
*26 February – The first edition of ''[[The Sun on Sunday]]'' is published.
*27 February – Singer [[Charlotte Church]] and her parents agree damages and costs of £600,000 with [[Rupert Murdoch]]'s News Group, publishers of the defunct ''[[News of the World]]'', after the newspaper printed stories about them from information gained through [[News of the World phone hacking scandal|phone hacking activities]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-17178550 |work=BBC News |title=Charlotte Church 'sickened' by NoW phone hacking |publisher=BBC |date=27 February 2012 |accessdate=28 February 2012}}</ref>
*29 February –
**[[James Murdoch]] resigns from [[News International]] to focus on running [[News Limited]]'s television business with the [[News International phone hacking scandal]] as a factor in the decision.<ref>{{cite news|author = |url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/phone-hacking/9114105/James-Murdoch-resigns-from-News-International-as-firm-admits-papers-could-be-sold-off.html |title=James Murdoch resigns from News International as firm admits papers could be sold off |publisher=Telegraph Media Group |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |date=29 February 2012 |accessdate=1 March 2012}}</ref>
**David Rathband, the policeman blinded by gunman Raoul Moat during a [[2010 Northumbria Police manhunt|2010 shooting]] is found dead at his home in Blyth, Northumberland.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-tyne-17216389 |title=BBC News – Raoul Moat victim PC David Rathband found dead at home |publisher=BBC |work=BBC News |date=1 March 2012 |accessdate=1 March 2012}}</ref>

*3 March  – A meteor is seen over most of the United Kingdom at about 21:40 GMT.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-17248959 |title=BBC News – Meteor witnessed across Britain |publisher=Bbc.co.uk |date= |accessdate=2012-03-07}}</ref>
*14 March – It is announced that the towns of [[Chelmsford]], [[Perth, Scotland|Perth]] and [[St Asaph]] are being granted [[City status in the United Kingdom|city status]] to mark the [[Diamond Jubilee of Elizabeth II]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-17364651|work=BBC News|title=Three towns win city status for Diamond Jubilee|publisher=BBC|date=14 March 2012|accessdate=14 March 2012}}</ref>
*15 March – Unemployment now stands at a 17-year high of nearly 2,700,000 for January.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-17379243|work=BBC News|title=UK economy: Jobs|publisher=BBC|date=15 March 2012|accessdate=17 March 2012}}</ref>

He will subsequently take up the role as [[Master of Magdalene College, Cambridge]].<ref>{{cite news|first=Robert|last=Pigott|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-17399403|work=BBC News|title=Archbishop of Canterbury Rowan Williams to stand down|publisher=BBC|date=16 March 2012|accessdate=16 March 2012}}</ref>
*17 March – Personal documents released from the papers of [[Margaret Thatcher]] show the former [[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|British Prime Minister]] held a private meeting with [[Rupert Murdoch]] at [[Chequers]] weeks before his purchase of [[Times Newspapers]] in 1981.<ref>{{cite news|last=Wallis |first=Holly |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-17366040 |work=BBC News |title=Thatcher files: Secret Murdoch meeting detailed in archives |publisher=BBC |date=17 March 2012 |accessdate=17 March 2012}}</ref>
*18 March – British journalists Gareth Montgomery-Johnson and Nicholas Davies-Jones, detained last month in [[Libya]] after being accused of entering the country illegally, have been released the country's Interior Ministry confirms.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-17424831 |work=BBC News |title=British Press TV journalists freed by Libya |publisher=BBC |date=18 March 2012 |accessdate=18 March 2012}}</ref>
* 21 March, [[George Osborne]] delivers his 2012 United Kingdom Budget.
*24 March – ''[[The Sunday Times]]'' releases a video showing [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party]] co-treasurer [[Peter Cruddas]] allegedly offering undercover reporters access to Prime Minister [[David Cameron]] for £250,000.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.sky.com/home/politics/article/16195702 |title=Conservatives Deny Sunday Times 'Cash For Access' |work=Sky News |publisher=British Sky Broadcasting |date=24 March 2012 |accessdate=24 March 2012}}</ref>
*25 March – Peter Cruddas resigns as Tory Party co-treasurer following the "Cash for Access" revelations.<ref>{{cite news|first=Daniel |last=Boffey |url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2012/mar/25/peter-cruddas-resigns-cash-access |title=Senior Tory Peter Cruddas resigns after cash for PM access sting |newspaper=The Observer |publisher=Guardian Media Group |date=25 March 2012 |accessdate=25 March 2012}}</ref>
*26 March –
**In the wake of the [[2012 United Kingdom cash for access scandal|Cash for Access]] scandal, [[David Cameron]] publishes details of [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party]] donors who have had dinner with him at [[10 Downing Street]].<ref>{{cite news|first=Nick |last=Robinson |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-17508271 |work=BBC News |title=David Cameron publishes list of all donors dining at No 10 |publisher=BBC |date=26 March 2012 |accessdate=27 March 2012}}</ref>
**Fuel tanker drivers belonging to the [[Unite the Union|Unite union]] vote overwhelmingly to take [[strike action]] in a dispute over terms and conditions.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-17512729 |work=BBC News |title=Fuel strike drivers vote yes in row over conditions |publisher=BBC |date=26 March 2012 |accessdate=27 March 2012}}</ref>
*27 March – The cost of a first-class stamp will rise from 46p to 60p from 30 April while second class post will increase from 36p to 50p after regulator [[Ofcom]] lifts some price controls on [[Royal Mail]].<ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-17522500|title= Stamp prices: First-class stamps to cost 60p|publisher= BBC|work= BBC News|date= 27 March 2012|accessdate= 27 March 2012}}</ref>
*29 March – [[Respect Party]] candidate [[George Galloway]] wins the [[Bradford West by-election]], taking the seat from the [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour]] with a majority of 10,140 votes.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-17549388 |work=BBC News |title=George Galloway wins Bradford West by-election |publisher=BBC |date=30 March 2012 |accessdate=30 March 2012}}</ref>

===April===
*7 April – The 158th [[The Boat Race|University Boat Race]] between Oxford and Cambridge is stopped mid-race due to a swimmer in the water. Cambridge go on to win when a clash of oars at the restart leaves Oxford with a broken paddle.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/rowing/17645929 |work=BBC Sport |title=Boat Race: Oxford & Cambridge interrupted by swimmer |publisher=BBC |date=7 April 2012 |accessdate=7 April 2012}}</ref>
*12 April – [[Transport for London]] bans an advertising campaign due to run on buses by a Christian group; which was suggesting that gay people could be cured by therapy.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-london-17693947|title=London transport chiefs ban 'gay cure' bus advert|publisher=BBC|work=BBC News|date=12 April 2012|accessdate=13 April 2012}}</ref>
*15 April – Centennial anniversary of the sinking of [[RMS Titanic]] is commemorated in [[Southampton]], [[Halifax Regional Municipality|Halifax, Nova Scotia]], and around the world.
*18 April – In a rare move, television cameras are allowed into [[High Court of Justiciary|the High Court in Edinburgh]] to film the sentencing of David Gilroy for the [[murder of Suzanne Pilley]].<ref>{{cite news|first=Steven|last=Brocklehurst|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-edinburgh-east-fife-17754517|title=Filming in court 'not precedent'|publisher=BBC|work=BBC News|date=18 April 2012|accessdate=18 April 2012}}</ref>
*20 April – [[Belfast]] MAC (Metropolitan Arts Centre), designed by [[Hackett Hall McKnight]], opens in [[Northern Ireland]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Belfast's new arts venue the MAC prepares to open|work=BBC News|date=2012-04-19|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-17770503|accessdate=2014-10-27}}</ref>
*22 April – 30-year-old Claire Squires collapses and dies while running the [[London Marathon]], the tenth death in the race's history. She had planned to raise £500 for [[The Samaritans]], but within a day members of the public have donated £219,000.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2012/apr/23/london-marathon-death-claire-squires|title=London marathon death: donations rocket for runner Claire Squires|publisher= Guardian Media Group|newspaper= The Guardian|date=23 April 2012|accessdate=24 April 2012}}</ref> The end of the year would see this total rise to nearly £1M.<ref>{{cite web|title=Claire Squires' Just Giving fundraising page|url=http://www.justgiving.com/Claire-Squires2/|accessdate=15 December 2012}}</ref>
*25 April – Figures from the [[Office for National Statistics]] indicate the [[Economy of the United Kingdom|UK economy]] has returned to recession after shrinking by 0.2% in the first three months of 2012, bringing about a much-feared [[double-dip recession]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-17836624 |work=BBC News |title=UK economy in double-dip recession |publisher=BBC |date=25 April 2012 |accessdate=25 April 2012}}</ref>
*30 April – Figures released by the [[Met Office]] show that April was the wettest on record in the United Kingdom.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-17897982 |work=BBC News |title=2012 had wettest April for 100 years, Met Office says |publisher=BBC |date=30 April 2012 |accessdate=18 May 2012}}</ref>

===May===
* 3 May – [[United Kingdom local elections, 2012|Local elections]] held in England, [[Scottish local elections, 2012|Scotland]] and Wales.
* 4 May –
**Local elections result in Labour making gains and winning the largest number of councillors in contested seats in England<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/special/vote2012/council/england.stm |title=Vote 2012: English Council Results |publisher=BBC |work=BBC News |date= |accessdate=18 May 2012}}</ref> and Wales<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/special/vote2012/council/wales.stm |title=Vote 2012: Welsh Council Results |publisher=BBC |work=BBC News |date= |accessdate=18 May 2012}}</ref> and the SNP making gains and winning the largest number of councillors in Scotland.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/special/vote2012/council/scotland.stm |title=Vote 2012: Scottish Council Results |publisher=BBC |work=BBC News |date= |accessdate=18 May 2012}}</ref> The estimated voting share was: 39% Labour, 31% Conservative, 16% [[Liberal Democrats (UK)|Liberal Democrats]] and 14% other.<ref>{{cite news|last=Sparrow |first=Andrew |url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2012/may/03/local-election-results-live-coverage?newsfeed=true |title=Local election results – live coverage |newspaper=The Guardian |publisher=Guardian Media Group |date=3 May 2012 |accessdate=18 May 2012}}</ref>
**[[Boris Johnson]] is re-elected as [[Mayor of London]] with 51.5% of the vote.<ref>{{cite news|first=Louise |last=Stewart |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-17968021 |work=BBC News |title=David Cameron hails Boris Johnson's London mayoral win |publisher=BBC |date=5 May 2012 |accessdate=18 May 2012}}</ref> [[Ken Livingston]] subsequently says it would be his 'last election'.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-17968483 |work=BBC News |title=Ken Livingstone: End of the road for political heavyweight |publisher=BBC |date=5 May 2012 |accessdate=18 May 2012}}</ref> In the [[London Assembly]], Labour becomes the party with the greatest number of seats, with minor losses for the Conservatives and Liberal Democrats. The British National Party loses its only seat. Of 25 seats, the final tally stands at: Labour 12, Conservatives 9, Liberal Democrats 2, Green 2.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/special/vote2012/assembly/|title=Vote 2012:London Assembly Results|publisher=BBC|work=BBC News|year=2012|accessdate=18 May 2012}}</ref>
* 5 May – [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] win the seventh [[FA Cup]] of their history with a 2–1 win over [[Liverpool F.C.|Liverpool]] in the [[2012 FA Cup Final|final]].<ref>{{cite news|last=McNulty |first=Phil |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/17878435 |title=BBC Sport – Chelsea 2-1 Liverpool |publisher=Bbc.co.uk |date=5 May 2012 |accessdate=2012-06-11}}</ref>
* 13 May – [[Manchester City F.C.|Manchester City]] win the Premier League title on goal difference ahead of [[Manchester|cross city]] [[Manchester derby|rivals]] [[Manchester United F.C.|United]], their first top division title since 1968.<ref>{{cite news|author = |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/18052553 |work=BBC Sport |title=Man City win Premier League: BBC Sport pundits reaction |publisher=BBC |date=13 May 2012 |accessdate=11 June 2012}}</ref>
* 16 May – The [[Office for National Statistics]] publishes figures which show that unemployment has fallen by 45,000 in the three months to March to 2,625,000, a rate of 8.2%. Youth unemployment has fallen to 1,020,000, a rate of 21.9%. Average weekly pay, including bonuses, grew by 0.6%. The claimant count dropped by 13,700 in April to 1,590,000, and the March figure is revised to show a fall of 5,400 rather than a rise of 3,600.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-18084679 |work=BBC News |title=UK unemployment falls by 45,000 |publisher=BBC |date=16 May 2012 |accessdate=18 May 2012}}</ref>
* 18 May – The [[Olympic Flame]] arrives in [[Cornwall]] on board a flight from Athens, ready for the torch relay ahead of the [[2012 Summer Olympics|London 2012 games]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Heald|first=Claire|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-18093410 |work=BBC News|title=Olympic torch: Flame arrives in UK for 2012 torch relay|publisher=BBC|date=18 May 2012|accessdate=18 May 2012}}</ref>
* 19 May – [[Chelsea F.C.|Chelsea]] win the [[European Cup]] for the first time in their history, defeating [[Bayern Munich]] of Germany on penalties after a 1–1 draw in [[Munich|Munich's]] [[Allianz Arena]].<ref>{{cite news|last=McNulty |first=Phil |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/18044385 |work=BBC Sport |title=Chelsea 1-1 Bayern Munich (aet, 4-3 pens) |publisher=BBC |date=19 May 2012 |accessdate=11 June 2012}}</ref>
* 22 May – The [[Royal Navy]]'s first female warship commander, Commander [[Sarah West]], takes up her post on [[HMS Portland (F79)|HMS ''Portland'']] at [[Rosyth]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-devon-14451761|title=Royal Navy appoints first woman warship commander|publisher=BBC|work=BBC News|date=8 August 2011|accessdate=8 August 2011}}</ref>
* 25 May – Millionaire's daughter Laura Johnson, who drove looters around London during the [[2011 England riots|2011 riots]] is jailed for two years.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-london-18203338|title= London riots: Millionaire's daughter Laura Johnson jailed|publisher= BBC|work= BBC News|date= 25 May 2012|accessdate= 25 May 2012}}</ref>

*24 June – In the Euro 2012 football tournament's quarter-final, [[England national football team|England]] lose by a [[Penalty shoot-out (association football)|penalty shoot-out]], with former [[West Ham United F.C.|West Ham]] player [[Alessandro Diamanti]] converting from the spot at the [[Olimpiyskiy National Sports Complex]] in [[Kiev]] to take [[Italy national football team|Italy]] through to the semi-final against [[Germany national football team|Germany]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/17875864 |work=BBC Sport |title=Euro 2012 quarter-final: England v Italy |publisher=BBC |date=24 June 2012 |accessdate=4 July 2012}}</ref>

*28 June – A man is killed as [[2012 Great Britain and Ireland floods|torrential rain causes widespread flooding]] across England. The storms also force the [[2012 Summer Olympics torch relay|Olympic torch relay]] to be halted briefly.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-18624085 |work=BBC News |title=Flash flooding: Man found dead as downpours cause chaos |publisher=BBC |date=28 June 2012 |accessdate=4 July 2012}}</ref> Both main railway lines connecting England and [[Scotland]] are closed after the tracks are blocked by landslides.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-south-scotland-18623801 |work=BBC News |title=Landslides and fire disrupt rail services as rains hit Scotland |publisher=BBC |date=29 June 2012 |accessdate=4 July 2012}}</ref>
*30 June – The [[Government of the United Kingdom|UK government]] announces an independent review of the workings of the [[Libor]] inter-bank lending rate in the wake of the [[Barclays]] scandal.<ref>{{cite news|author = |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-18640916 |work=BBC News |title=Ministers to order Libor bank rate review |publisher=BBC |date=30 June 2012 |accessdate=4 July 2012}}</ref>
