== Events ==

=== January ===
*1 January – The United Kingdom assumes presidency of [[G8]] group. [[39th G8 summit|Next G8 summit]] is to be held on the shores of [[Lough Erne]], [[County Fermanagh]] in June 2013.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-20880368 |work=BBC News |title=UK assumes presidency of G8 group |publisher=BBC |date=1 January 2013 |accessdate=4 January 2013}}</ref>
*4 January – Mark Cahill, a 51-year-old former pub landlord from [[West Yorkshire]] becomes the first person in the UK to receive a [[hand transplant]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Gallagher |first=James |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/health-20898940 |work=BBC News |title=UK's first hand transplant operation |publisher=BBC |date=4 January 2013 |accessdate=4 January 2013}}</ref>
*10 January – [[April Casburn]], a senior detective with the [[Metropolitan Police]] is found guilty of trying to sell information on the investigation into [[News of the World phone hacking scandal|phone hacking]] to the ''[[News of the World]]'', the newspaper at the centre of the scandal.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-20976214 |work=BBC News |title=April Casburn guilty of leak to News of the World offer |publisher=BBC |date=10 January 2013 |accessdate=10 January 2013}}</ref>
*11 January – Publication of the ''[[Giving Victims a Voice]]'' report, initiated as a result of publicity surrounding the [[Jimmy Savile sexual abuse scandal]]
*16 January – A [[2013 Vauxhall helicopter crash|helicopter crash]] in central London kills two people and injures 13 others.<ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-21040410|title= London helicopter crash: Two die in Vauxhall crane accident|publisher= BBC|work= BBC News|date= 16 January 2013|accessdate= 16 January 2013}}</ref>
*18 January - Snow falls across parts of the UK & Ireland during the January 2013 Great Britain and Ireland Snowfall, what goes on until January 23
*19 January – An [[avalanche]] at [[Glen Coe|Glencoe]] in [[Argyll and Bute]], [[Scotland]], kills four people.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/topics/weather/9813298/Four-dead-and-one-seriously-injured-in-Glencoe-avalanche.html|title=Four dead and one seriously injured in Glencoe avalanche|work=The Telegraph|location=London|first=David|last=Barrett|date=19 January 2013|accessdate=27 October 2013}}</ref>
*22 January – A death sentence handed to British citizen [[Lindsay Sandiford]] by an Indonesian court for drug smuggling is condemned by the UK government.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.channel4.com/news/british-woman-sentenced-to-death-in-bali |title=Government condemns Sandiford death penalty |work=Channel 4 News |publisher=ITN |date=22 January 2013 |accessdate=23 January 2013}}</ref>
*28 January – Government plans to [[Sixth Periodic Review of Westminster constituencies|redraw constituency boundaries]] before the [[United Kingdom general election, 2015|next general election]] are defeated in the [[House of Commons (United Kingdom)|House of Commons]] by 334 to 292.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-21235169 |work=BBC News |title=Conservatives lose boundary review vote |publisher=BBC |date=29 January 2013 |accessdate=29 January 2013}}</ref>
*30 January – Tony McCluskie is found guilty of the March 2012 murder of his sister, the actress [[Gemma McCluskie]] and [[life sentence|jailed for life]] with a recommendation he serve a minimum term of 20 years.<ref name="brother">{{cite news|title=EastEnders' Gemma McCluskie death: Brother faces murder trial|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-london-20640533|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=28 September 2012|accessdate=28 September 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-2270723/BREAKING-NEWS-Brother-Eastenders-actress-Gemma-McCluskie-guilty-murdering-throwing-body-canal.html |title=Brother of EastEnders actress Gemma McCluskie found guilty of murdering her and dismembering her body|publisher=Daily Mail and General Trust|newspaper=Daily Mail |date=30 January 2013 |accessdate=30 January 2013 |location=London |first=Chris |last=Greenwood}}</ref>

=== February ===
*1 February – Metropolitan Police detective April Casburn is jailed for fifteen months.<ref>{{cite news|author=Dominic Casciani |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-21292338 |work=BBC News |title=April Casburn jailed for News of the World leak offer |publisher=BBC |date=1 February 2013 |accessdate=1 February 2013}}</ref>
*4 February – Former [[Government of the United Kingdom|government]] Minister [[Chris Huhne]] [[R v Huhne and Pryce|pleads guilty]] to [[perverting the course of justice]] over claims he caused his ex-wife to accept [[Point system (driving)|speeding points]] he had incurred. He also announces his intention to [[Resignation from the British House of Commons|resign]] his [[House of Commons (United Kingdom)|House of Commons]] seat.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-21320992 |work=BBC News |title=Chris Huhne admits perverting the course of justice |publisher=BBC |date=4 February 2013 |accessdate=8 February 2013}}</ref>
*5 February – The House of Commons votes 400 to 175 in favour of a vote on the bill to legalise [[same-sex marriage]] in [[England and Wales]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Craig |first=Jon |url=http://news.sky.com/story/1048058/gay-marriage-mps-back-bill-in-commons-vote| |title=Gay Marriage: MPs Back Bill In Commons Vote |publisher=British Sky Broadcasting |work=Sky News |date=5 February 2013 |accessdate=7 February 2013}}</ref>
*7 February – [[Secretary of State for Education]] [[Michael Gove]] confirms that plans to replace the [[General Certificate of Secondary Education]] with a new [[English Baccalaureate]] qualification have been abandoned.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-21363396 |work=BBC News |title=Planned switch from GCSEs to Baccalaureate in England 'abandoned' |publisher=BBC |date=7 February 2013 |accessdate=7 February 2013}}</ref>
*23 February – UK loses top AAA credit rating for first time since 1978 after being downgraded by the ratings agency Moody's<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-21554311 |work=BBC News |title=UK loses top AAA credit rating for first time since 1978 |publisher=BBC |date=23 February 2013 |accessdate=23 February 2013}}</ref>
*25 February – [[Cardinal (Catholicism)|Cardinal]] [[Keith O'Brien]], Britain's most senior [[Roman Catholicism|Roman Catholic]] cleric, resigns as the [[Archbishop of St. Andrews and Edinburgh]] due to allegations of [[Sexual abuse|inappropriate]] [[Human sexual activity|sexual behaviour]] towards [[priests]] in the 1980s.<ref>{{cite news|last=Pigott |first=Robert |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-21572724 |work=BBC News |title=Cardinal Keith O'Brien resigns as Archbishop |publisher=BBC |date=25 February 2013 |accessdate=3 March 2013}}</ref>
*27 February – [[Archbishop of Glasgow|Archbishop]] of [[Glasgow]] [[Philip Tartaglia]] is appointed [[apostolic administrator]] of the [[Roman Catholic]] [[Archdiocese of St Andrews and Edinburgh]] following the resignation of [[Cardinal (Catholicism)|Cardinal]] [[Keith O'Brien]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-21605525 |work=BBC News |title=Pope appoints temporary replacement to Cardinal Keith O'Brien |publisher=BBC |date=27 February 2013 |accessdate=3 March 2013}}</ref>
*28 February – The [[Eastleigh by-election, 2013|Eastleigh by-election]], which was triggered by the resignation of Chris Huhne, sees the Liberal Democrats hold the seat despite a UKIP surge that pushes the Conservatives into third place.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-21625726 |work=BBC News |title=Eastleigh by-election: Lib Dems hold on despite UKIP surge |publisher=BBC |date=1 March 2013 |accessdate=1 March 2013}}</ref>

**[[Secretary of State for Foreign Affairs|Foreign Secretary]] [[William Hague]] announced the UK will send armoured vehicles and body armour to opposition forces in [[Syria]] to help save lives.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-21684105 |work=BBC News |title=UK to send armoured vehicles to Syrian opposition |publisher=BBC |date=6 March 2013 |accessdate=6 March 2013}}</ref>

**Prime Minister [[David Cameron]] says talks between himself and the leaders of the UK's other main political parties on the recommendations of the [[Leveson report]] have broken down, and that he intends to publish a [[Royal Charter]] on press regulations.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-21785611 |work=BBC News |title=David Cameron halts press regulation talks |publisher=BBC |date=14 March 2013 |accessdate=15 March 2013}}</ref>

*16 March –
**Lance Corporal [[James Ashworth]], who died in southern [[Afghanistan]] in 2012 while protecting his colleagues from a grenade blast is to be awarded the [[Victoria Cross]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-21810581 |work=BBC News |title=Victoria Cross for Afghan hero L/Cpl James Ashworth |publisher=BBC |date=16 March 2013 |accessdate=16 March 2013}}</ref>

**The date for the [[Scottish independence referendum, 2014|Scottish independence referendum]] is announced as 18 September 2014.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-21828424 |work=BBC News |title=Scottish independence: Referendum to be held on 18 September, 2014 |publisher=BBC |date=21 March 2013 |accessdate=21 March 2013}}</ref>

**Former [[Secretary of State for Foreign and Commonwealth Affairs|Foreign Secretary]] [[David Miliband]] confirms he is to resign as an [[Member of Parliament|MP]] and move to the United States to become head of the [[International Rescue Committee]] in New York.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-21947497 |work=BBC News |title=David Miliband to step down as MP |publisher=BBC |date=26 March 2013 |accessdate=27 March 2013}}</ref>

===May===
*2 May – [[James McCormick (businessman)|James McCormick]] is jailed for ten years at the [[Old Bailey]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-22380368 |work=BBC News |title=Fake bomb detector seller James McCormick jailed |publisher=BBC |date=2 May 2013 |accessdate=2 May 2013}}</ref>

**Results from yesterday's [[United Kingdom local elections, 2013|local elections]] showed gains for the [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour]] and [[UKIP]] and losses for the [[Liberal Democrats (UK)|Liberal Democrats]] and the [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-22382098 |work=BBC News |title=Local elections: Nigel Farage hails results as a 'game changer' |publisher=BBC |date=3 May 2013 |accessdate=8 May 2013}}</ref>
**[[Labour Party (UK)|Labour]]'s [[Emma Lewell-Buck]] retains the [[South Shields (UK Parliament constituency)|South Shields]] Parliamentary seat for the party following the previous day's [[South Shields by-election, 2013|by-election]] triggered by the resignation of [[David Miliband]]. However, Labour's majority is reduced significantly after the [[UK Independence Party]] secures almost a quarter of the votes cast.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-22393094 |work=BBC News |title=South Shields by-election: Labour wins as UKIP makes big gains |publisher=BBC |date=3 May 2013 |accessdate=3 May 2013}}</ref>
*8 May – Sir [[Alex Ferguson]], the most successful manager in English football during his 27 years in charge of Manchester United, announces his retirement as the Manager of [[Manchester United]] after the end of the Premier League season later in the month.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/22447018 |work=BBC Sport |title=Sir Alex Ferguson to retire as Manchester United manager |publisher=BBC |date=8 May 2013 |accessdate=8 May 2013}}</ref>

*13 May – Stuart Hazell, 38, admits the [[Murder of Tia Sharp]], 12-year-old granddaughter of his partner Christine Sharp, who was found dead in [[New Addington]], London, nine months ago. His murder trial began six days ago but he had previously denied the charge against him.
*14 May –
** – The [[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative Party]] publishes a draft European Union (Referendum) Bill aimed at holding a referendum on Britain's membership of the [[European Union]] by 2017. Prime Minister [[David Cameron]] had previously said a referendum would be held if he could renegotiate the terms of Britain's EU membership, but Tory MPs were unhappy that legislation for a referendum was not included in the recent [[Speech from the throne|Queen's Speech]].<ref>{{cite news|author=Nick Robinson |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-22530655 |work=BBC News |title=David Cameron: EU referendum bill shows only Tories listen |publisher=BBC |date=14 May 2013 |accessdate=14 May 2013}}</ref>
** – Stuart Hazell is sentenced to life imprisonment at the [[Old Bailey]] with a recommended minimum term of 38 years.
*15 May –
**In the [[House of Commons (UK)|House of Commons]], an amendment to the [[Speech from the throne|Queen's Speech]] expressing regret that it did not contain legislation for a referendum on Britain's EU membership is defeated 277–131.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-22547910 |work=BBC News |title=More than 100 Tory MPs 'express regret' at lack of referendum bill |publisher=BBC |date=15 May 2013 |accessdate=16 May 2013}}</ref>
**MPs debate government proposals to tighten the [[Dangerous Dogs Act 1991|law governing dangerous dogs]] following the death of Jade Anderson in March. The legislation would give police greater powers to deal with attacks on private property.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-manchester-22556348 |work=BBC News |title=Jade Anderson death: New dog laws 'will not prevent tragedies' |publisher=BBC |date=16 May 2013 |accessdate=25 May 2013}}</ref>
*16 May – UKIP leader [[Nigel Farage]] is heckled by angry protesters during a campaign visit to [[Edinburgh]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-22559526 |work=BBC News |title=Farage swarmed by angry protesters in Edinburgh |publisher=BBC |date=16 May 2013 |accessdate=17 May 2013}}</ref>
*21 May – MPs vote 366–161 in favour of the [[Marriage (Same Sex Couples) Bill]] taking an important step towards allowing [[Same-sex marriage in the United Kingdom|gay marriage in England and Wales]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-22605011 |work=BBC News |title=Gay marriage: Commons passes Cameron's plan |publisher=BBC |date=21 May 2013 |accessdate=21 May 2013}}</ref>
*22 May – In [[Woolwich]], [[Murder of Lee Rigby|Fusilier Lee Rigby is killed in the street]]. Two men carrying knives and a meat cleaver are subsequently shot and apprehended by police. The government treats the killing as a terrorist incident.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-22630303 |work=BBC News |title=Woolwich machete attack leaves man dead |publisher=BBC |date=22 May 2013 |accessdate=23 May 2013}}</ref>
*23 May – The victim of the Woolwich attack is confirmed to have been a serving British soldier, and named as Drummer Lee Rigby of the [[2nd Battalion the Royal Regiment of Fusiliers]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-22644857 |work=BBC News |title=Woolwich attack: Lee Rigby named as victim |publisher=BBC |date=23 May 2013 |accessdate=23 May 2013}}</ref>
*24 May – A 24-year-old zoo worker is seriously injured after being attacked by a [[Sumatran tiger]] at [[South Lakes Safari Zoo|South Lakes Wild Animal Park]] in Cumbria.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-22661642 |work==BBC News |title=Tiger attacks woman at South Lakes Wild Animal Park |publisher=BBC |date=24 May 2013 |accessdate=25 May 2013}}</ref> Sarah McClay subsequently dies in hospital as a result of her injuries. Police launch an investigation into the attack.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-cumbria-22664613 |work=BBC News |title=Cumbria Tiger attack: Sarah McClay dies of her injuries |publisher=BBC |date=25 May 2013 |accessdate=25 May 2013}}</ref>
*25 May – 48 people are rescued from a boat which begins taking on water after colliding with a rock off the coast of [[Pembrokeshire]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-wales-south-west-wales-22666265 |work=BBC News |title=48 people rescue from sinking boat near Skomer Island |publisher=BBC |date=25 May 2013 |accessdate=25 May 2013}}</ref>
*30 May – A court in [[Mold, Flintshire|Mold]], [[Wales]], finds Mark Bridger guilty of abducting and murdering five-year-old [[Murder of April Jones|April Jones]] in October 2012. The trial judge recommends that 47-year-old Bridger should never be released from prison.
