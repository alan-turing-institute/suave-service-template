*[[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|Prime Minister]] – [[David Cameron]] ([[Coalition Government 2010-2015|Coalition]])

==Events==

===January===
*3 January – [[Cyclone Anne (2014)|Strong winds and high tides]] bring flooding to large parts of Western England, Wales and Scotland.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-25584221 |work=BBC News |title=Flooding continues to threaten UK |publisher=BBC |date=3 January 2014 |accessdate=4 January 2014}}</ref>
*7 January – Four people are killed when a [[United States Air Force]] Sikorsky [[HH-60G Pave Hawk]] helicopter, based at [[RAF Lakenheath]] in Suffolk, crashes at a nature reserve in [[Cley next the Sea]], north Norfolk.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-25646978 |work=BBC News |title=Norfolk helicopter crash: Four people dead |publisher=BBC |date=8 January 2014 |accessdate=8 January 2014}}</ref>
*8 January – An inquest jury decides that [[Death of Mark Duggan|Mark Duggan]], whose death sparked the [[2011 England riots]] was lawfully killed by police.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-25657949 |work=BBC News |title=Mark Duggan inquest: Family fury at lawful killing decision |publisher=BBC |date=8 January 2014 |accessdate=9 January 2014}}</ref>
*10 January – At the [[Old Bailey]], police officer Keith Wallis pleads guilty to misconduct in a public office over an email he sent to his local MP concerning the [[Plebgate]] affair.<ref>{{cite news| last1=Davenport| first1=Justin| last2=Cecil| first2=Nicholas| date= 10 January 2014| title=Plebgate scandal: Met police apology to Andrew Mitchell after officer admits lie| url=http://www.standard.co.uk/news/uk/plebgate-scandal-met-police-apology-to-andrew-mitchell-after-officer-admits-lie-9051453.html| newspaper=Evening Standard|location=London| publisher=Evening Standard Limited}}</ref>
*12 January – [[Vincent Nichols]], the [[Roman Catholicism in the United Kingdom|Roman Catholic]] [[Archbishop of Westminster]] is among 19 senior Catholic clergy who will be created [[Cardinal (Catholicism)|Cardinal]]s by [[Pope Francis]] on 22 February, it is announced.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-25703940 |work=BBC News |title=Archbishop of Westminster Vincent Nichols to become Cardinal |publisher=BBC |date=12 January 2014 |accessdate=12 January 2014}}</ref>
*13 January – The UK Treasury says that should Scots vote to leave the UK, it will honour all UK government debt issued up to the date of Scottish independence.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-25707218 |work=BBC News |title=Scottish independence: Treasury makes debt pledge |publisher=BBC |date=13 January 2014 |accessdate=13 January 2014}}</ref>
*15 January – [[Birmingham City Council]] could be forced to sell off some of its assets to pay £1bn of legal claims over equality.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-birmingham-25733267 |work=BBC News |title=Birmingham landmarks including the NEC could be sold off |publisher=BBC |date=15 January 2014 |accessdate=16 January 2014}}</ref>
*16 January – 
**Sir [[Peter Fahy]], the Chief Constable of [[Greater Manchester Police]] will face prosecution over safety breaches after an unarmed man was shot dead in Cheshire in March 2012.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-25759909 |work=BBC News |title=Greater Manchester Police chief faces shooting charges |publisher=BBC |date=16 January 2014 |accessdate=16 January 2014}}</ref>
**[[Chancellor of the Exchequer|Chancellor]] [[George Osborne]] tells BBC News he wants to see a rise in the minimum wage above the rate of inflation.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-25766558 |work=BBC News |title=Osborne wants above-inflation minimum wage rise |publisher=BBC |date=16 January 2014 |accessdate=16 January 2014}}</ref>
*18 January –
**The [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour Party]] confirms that Del Singh, a candidate for the forthcoming European elections, was among two Britons killed during a suicide bombing at a restaurant in the Afghan capital, Kabul the previous day.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-25788623 |work=BBC News |title=Two Britons killed in Kabul suicide attack on restaurant |publisher=BBC |date=18 January 2014 |accessdate=18 January 2014}}</ref>
**16-year-old Lewis Clarke of Bristol sets a new world record after becoming the youngest person to trek to the [[South Pole]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-bristol-25794003 |work=BBC News |title=Lewis Clarke: Teenager arrives at Amundsen-Scott South Pole Station |publisher=BBC |date=18 January 2014 |accessdate=19 January 2014}}</ref>
*19 January – The [[UK Independence Party]] suspends an Oxfordshire councillor who blamed the floods that hit the country earlier in the month on the government's decision to legalise same-sex marriage because it had angered God.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-25802437 |work=BBC News |title=UKIP suspends councillor who blamed flooding on gay marriage |publisher=BBC |date=19 January 2014 |accessdate=19 January 2014}}</ref>
*22 January – UK unemployment falls to 7.1%, surpassing economic forecasts and placing pressure on the [[Bank of England]] to raise interest rates. The bank, which said it would consider an increase once unemployment reached 7% says it has no immediate plans to introduce a raise.<ref>{{cite news|first=Angela |last=Monaghan |url=https://www.theguardian.com/business/2014/jan/22/uk-unemployment-rate-forward-guidance-rate-rise |title=UK unemployment rate falls to 7.1% |publisher=Guardian Media Group |newspaper=The Guardian |date=22 January 2014 |accessdate=24 January 2014}}</ref>
*24 January – [[Sedgemoor District Council]] in [[Somerset]] declares a "major incident" in flooded areas as forecasters warn of more rain.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-somerset-25876309 |work=BBC News |title=Somerset floods: 'Major incident' declared |publisher=BBC |date=24 January 2014 |accessdate=24 January 2014}}</ref>
*25 January – Trees are uprooted and structural damaged caused to buildings by lightning as a heavy rainstorm hits the [[English Midlands|Midlands region]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-birmingham-25896795 |work=BBC News |title=West Bromwich church and houses hit by lightning in storm |publisher=BBC |date=25 January 2014 |accessdate=25 January 2014}}</ref>
*27 January – Research published by the [[Centre for Cities]] think tank suggests a widening economic gap between London and the rest of the UK, with ten times more jobs being created in the capital than elsewhere.<ref>{{cite news|first=Miranda |last=Prynne |url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/finance/jobs/10598690/Economic-gap-between-London-and-other-cities-widening-report-claims.html |title=Economic gap between London and other cities widening, report claims |publisher=Telegraph Media Group |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |date=27 January 2014 |accessdate=27 January 2014}}</ref>
*28 January – Figures released by the [[Office for National Statistics]] indicate the UK economy grew by 1.9% in 2013, its highest since 2007, but growth for the final quarter of the year was 0.7%.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-25926648 |work=BBC News |title=UK economy growing at fastest rate since 2007 |publisher=BBC |date=28 January 2014 |accessdate=28 January 2014}}</ref>
*29 January – During a visit to Scotland, [[Mark Carney]], Governor of the [[Bank of England]] says that in the event of Scottish independence, the country would need to give up some powers in return for a currency union with the United Kingdom.<ref>{{cite news|last=Black |first=Andrew |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-scotland-politics-25930075 |work=BBC News |title=Scottish independence: Carney says Scots currency plan may lead to power loss |publisher=BBC |date=29 January 2014 |accessdate=29 January 2014}}</ref>
*30 January – Figures released by the [[Met Office]] indicate Southern England and parts of the Midlands have experienced their highest January rainfall since records began in 1910. The announcement comes as military personnel prepare to help residents in flooded areas of Somerset.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-25944823 |work=BBC News |title=UK floods: January rain breaks records in parts of England |publisher=BBC |date=30 January 2014 |accessdate=30 January 2014}}</ref>
*31 January – The [[European Union (Referendum) Bill 2013-14]] is rejected by the House of Lords after peers vote not to allow more time for a debate, effectively killing off the proposed legislation.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-25977258 |work=BBC News |title=Cameron says Tories will bring back failed EU bill |publisher=BBC |date=31 January 2014 |accessdate=31 January 2014}}</ref>

===February===
*1 February – [[Sally Morgan, Baroness Morgan of Huyton|Sally Morgan]], the outgoing chair of [[Ofsted]] claims she is the victim of a "determined effort" by [[10 Downing Street]] to appoint more Conservatives to key public sector positions.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/education-25997102 |work=BBC News |title=Ofsted chair Sally Morgan accuses No 10 of ousting non-Tories from posts |publisher=BBC |date=1 February 2014 |accessdate=1 February 2014}}</ref>
*5 February – Part of the [[South Devon Railway sea wall]] carrying the [[Exeter to Plymouth Line|railway line]] linking [[London]] with the [[west of England]] is washed away by a powerful storm that has hit the UK overnight. Thousands of homes are also left without electricity.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-26042990 |work=BBC News |title=UK storms wash away railway line and leave thousands without power |publisher=BBC |date=5 February 2014 |accessdate=5 February 2014}}</ref> Prime Minister [[David Cameron]] announces that an extra £100 million will be spent on dealing with the aftermath of the floods that have hit the UK.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/news/local-national/uk/extra-100m-for-floods-aftermath-29980924.html |title=Extra £100m for floods aftermath |publisher=Independent News and Media |newspaper=Belfast Telegraph |date=5 February 2014 |accessdate=5 February 2014}}</ref>
*6 February – 
**PC Keith Wallis, who wrote to his MP falsely claiming to have witnessed the [[Plebgate]] incident in [[Downing Street]] is sentenced to twelve months imprisonment.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-26064536 |work=BBC News |title='Plebgate' PC Keith Wallis jailed for a year |publisher=BBC |date=6 February 2014 |accessdate=6 February 2014}}</ref>
**The [[Ministry of Defence (United Kingdom)|Ministry of Defence]] sends around 40 [[Royal Marines]] to the [[Somerset Levels]] to help with flood protection as more storms are expected. The Government also provides an extra £30 million for repairs.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-26061795 |work=BBC News |title=Prime minister pledges to get flood-hit UK back on the move |publisher=BBC |date=6 February 2014 |accessdate=6 February 2014}}</ref>
* 7–23 February – [[Great Britain at the 2014 Winter Olympics|Great Britain]] competes at the [[2014 Winter Olympics]] in [[Sochi]], [[Russia]] and wins 1 gold, one silver and two bronze medals. This is Great Britain's best performance at a winter games since 1924.
*8 February –
**Immigration minister [[Mark Harper]] resigns from the government after it was disclosed that his cleaner did not have permission to work in the UK.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-26101442 |work=BBC News |title=Immigration minister Mark Harper quits over cleaner's visa |publisher=BBC |date=8 February 2014 |accessdate=8 February 2014}}</ref>
**Rail links to [[South West England]] are cut off as fresh storms hit the area.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-26095937 |work=BBC News |title=UK storms: Coastal areas in west hit by renewed gales |publisher=Bbc.co.uk |date=8 February 2014 |accessdate=8 February 2014}}</ref>
*11 February – After visiting some of the country's flood hit areas, David Cameron says that "money is no object" as he announces measures to help those affected by the storms. He also warns that things may get worse before they get better. 1,600 troops are deployed to help in the relief effort, with more available if needed.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-26131515 |work=BBC News |title=UK floods: PM says money 'no object' in relief effort |publisher=BBC |date=11 February 2014 |accessdate=11 February 2014}}</ref>
*20 February – A 4.1 magnitude earthquake is recorded under the [[Bristol Channel]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-26277432 |work=BBC News |title=Earthquake confirmed in Bristol Channel |publisher=BBC |date=20 February 2014 |accessdate=20 February 2014}}</ref>
*22 February – [[Archbishop of Westminster]] [[Vincent Nichols]], leader of the [[Roman Catholic Church in England and Wales]], is created a [[Cardinal (Catholicism)|cardinal]] at a ceremony in [[Vatican City]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-26299425 |work=BBC News |title=Vincent Nichols becomes a cardinal in Rome ceremony |publisher=BBC |date=22 February 2014 |accessdate=22 February 2014}}</ref>
*25 February – A suspect in the 1982 IRA [[1982 Hyde Park bombing|Hyde Park bombing]] will not face trial after a judge ruled he cannot be prosecuted because he was mistakenly given an official assurance that he would not face trial. Some 182 letters have been issued as part of the [[Northern Ireland peace process]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-26342465 |work=BBC News |title=Alleged IRA Hyde Park bomber goes free after 'no trial' guarantee |publisher=BBC |date=25 February 2014 |accessdate=27 February 2014}}</ref>
*26 February – The two men convicted of the [[murder of Lee Rigby]] are sentenced to [[life imprisonment]], Michael Adebolajo without the possibility of parole, and Michael Adebowale with the possibility of parole after 45 years.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-26357007 |work=BBC News |title=Lee Rigby murder: Adebolajo and Adebowale jailed |publisher=BBC |date=26 February 2014 |accessdate=26 February 2014}}</ref>
*27 February – Prime Minister David Cameron appoints a judge to review the crisis over letters sent to paramilitary suspects advising them they would not be prosecuted after [[First Minister of Northern Ireland]] [[Peter Robinson (Northern Ireland politician)|Peter Robinson]] threatened to resign over the issue. The inquiry will present its findings by end of May.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-26373678 |work=BBC News |title=On the Runs: David Cameron orders NI suspects review |publisher=BBC |date=27 February 2014 |accessdate=27 February 2014}}</ref>
*28 February – Spree killer [[Joanne Dennehy]] is given a [[Sentencing in England and Wales|whole life sentence]] for three murders and two attempted murders committed in 2013.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-26389479 |work=BBC News |title=Spree killer Joanne Dennehy 'had sadistic lust for blood' |publisher=BBC |date=28 February 2014 |accessdate=28 February 2014}}</ref>

===March===
* 5 March – [[Birmingham City Council]] is to sell the [[NEC Group]] because the authority is facing legal claims over equal pay totalling more than £1bn.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-birmingham-26445914 |work=BBC News |title=Birmingham's NEC Group put up for sale |publisher=BBC |date=5 March 2014 |accessdate=5 March 2014}}</ref>
*6 March – 
**[[Prince Harry]] launches the [[Invictus Games (sport)|Invictus Games]], a [[Paralympic Games|Paralympic]]-style sporting championship for wounded soldiers.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-26467714 |work=BBC News|title=Prince Harry launches Paralympic-style games for soldiers |publisher=BBC |date=6 March 2014 |accessdate=6 March 2014}}</ref>
**[[Home Secretary]] [[Theresa May]] announces a public inquiry into undercover policing after revelations that officers spied on members of [[Murder of Stephen Lawrence|Stephen Lawrence]]'s family.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-26466867 |work=BBC News |title=Stephen Lawrence police 'spy' prompts public inquiry |publisher=BBC |date=6 March 2014 |accessdate=7 March 2014}}</ref>
*7–16 March – Great Britain finish 10th in the medal table of the [[2014 Winter Paralympics]], with six medals, the most successful Games since Innsbruck in [[1984 Winter Paralympics|1984]]. Four of Britain's medals were won by visually impaired skier [[Jade Etherington]], making her the greatest British Winter Paralympian of all-time.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/disability-sport/26602384 |work=BBC Sport |title=Sochi Paralympics: Games end with colourful ceremony |publisher=BBC |date=16 March 2014 |accessdate=9 April 2014}}</ref> 
*11 March – MPs vote 297–239 to allow the controversial Clause 119 element of the Care Bill that will allow ministers to close hospitals in an NHS trust if a neighbouring trust is in financial difficulty, even if the hospital concerned is performing well.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/health-26531807 |work=BBC News |title=MPs grant powers to close local hospitals |publisher=BBC |date=11 March 2014 |accessdate=11 March 2014}}</ref>
*18 March – [[Scottish Labour Party|Scottish Labour]]'s Devolution Commission publishes its long-awaited report setting out proposals for enhanced devolution that will be implemented if Scotland votes no in the referendum and Labour are elected in 2015.<ref name="Telegraph-2014-03-18">{{cite news|last=Johnson |first=Simon |url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/scotland/10705731/Labour-unveils-devolution-plans-to-tax-rich-Scots.html |title=Labour unveils devolution plans to tax rich Scots |publisher=Telegraph Media Group |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |date=18 March 2014 |accessdate=18 March 2014}}</ref><ref name="Scotsman-2014-03-18">{{cite news|last=Whitaker |first=Andrew |url=http://www.scotsman.com/news/politics/top-stories/labour-reveal-devolution-commission-findings-1-3344020 |title=Labour reveal Devolution Commission findings |publisher=Johnston Press |newspaper=The Scotsman |date=18 March 2014 |accessdate=18 March 2014}}</ref>
*19 March – As part of the [[2014 United Kingdom budget|2014 budget]], Chancellor [[George Osborne]] announces that a new [[One pound (British coin)|£1 coin]] will be introduced from 2017. Current £1 coins are vulnerable to counterfeiting, but the new 12-sided two-metal coin, based on the [[Threepence (British coin)|Threepenny bit]] will be more difficult to copy.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-26632863 |work=BBC News |title=New pound coin designed to combat counterfeiting |publisher=BBC |date=19 March 2014 |accessdate=22 March 2014}}</ref>
*28 March – BBC research suggests that less than 6% of social housing tenants affected by the [[under-occupancy penalty|bedroom tax]]{{mdash}}an aspect of the [[Welfare Reform Act 2012|2012 Welfare Reform Act]] that penalises tenants in receipt of [[Housing Benefit]] with spare bedrooms{{mdash}}have moved house as a result of the controversial measure.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-26770727 |work=BBC News |title=Housing benefits: Changes 'see 6% of tenants move' |publisher=BBC |date=28 March 2014 |accessdate=28 March 2014}}</ref>
*29 March – The first gay weddings take place in England and Wales following a change in the law in 2013 allowing same-sex marriage.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-26793127 |work=BBC News |title=Same-sex marriage now legal as first couples wed |publisher=BBC |date=29 March 2014 |accessdate=29 March 2014}}</ref>
*31 March – A jury is selected to hear a fresh inquest into the 96 deaths caused by the 1989 [[Hillsborough disaster]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-merseyside-26783600 |work=BBC News |title=Hillsborough inquests: Jury chosen to probe fans' deaths |publisher=BBC |date=31 March 2014 |accessdate=31 March 2014}}</ref>

===April===
*5 April – In [[horse racing]], [[Pineau De Re]], trained by Dr. [[Richard Newland (doctor)|Richard Newland]] and ridden by 37-year-old [[Leighton Aspell]], who returned to racing after coming out of retirement, wins the [[2014 Grand National]] at [[Aintree racecourse]] with the odds of [[Fractional odds|25/1]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/horse-racing/26903938 |work=BBC Sport |title=Grand National 2014: Pineau De Re wins Aintree race at 25–1|publisher=BBC|date=5 April 2014|accessdate=6 April 2014}}</ref>
*6 April –
**[[Sheffield Half Marathon|Sheffield's Half Marathon]] is cancelled because there were not enough water supplies for runners on the route.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-south-yorkshire-26910318 |work=BBC News |title=Sheffield Half Marathon cancelled due to lack of water for runners |publisher=BBC |date=6 April 2014 |accessdate=6 April 2014}}</ref>
**[[The Boat Race 2014]], contested between [[University of Oxford|Oxford]] and [[University of Cambridge|Cambridge University]] takes place; Oxford winning by 11 lengths (the biggest winning margin since 1973) after the Cambridge boat suffered damage to an oar early in the race.
*9 April – [[Maria Miller]] resigns of [[Secretary of State for Culture, Media and Sport|Culture Secretary]] following a row over her expenses, describing her decision as "the right thing to do". [[Sajid Javid]] is appointed as her successor.<ref>{{cite news|first=Andrew |last=Grice |url=http://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/politics/culture-secretary-maria-miller-resigns-amid-expenses-row-9247795.html |title=Maria Miller resigns: Sajid Javid appointed new Culture Secretary following expenses row |publisher=Independent Print Limited|newspaper=The Independent|date=9 April 2014|accessdate=9 April 2014}}</ref>
*20 April – The [[Anglican Diocese of Leeds|Diocese of Leeds]], created by merger of the [[Church of England]] [[diocese]]s of [[Diocese of Ripon|Ripon & Leeds]], [[Diocese of Bradford|Bradford]] and [[Diocese of Wakefield|Wakefield]], comes into being.<ref>{{cite web|title=New diocese of West Yorkshire and the Dales|url=http://www.churchofengland.org/media-centre/news/2014/04/new-diocese-of-west-yorkshire-and-the-dales.aspx|publisher=The Church of England|date=2014-04-20|accessdate=2014-05-08}}</ref>
*22 April – [[David Moyes]] is sacked as Manager of [[Manchester United F.C.|Manchester United]] ten months after succeeding [[Alex Ferguson]]. [[Ryan Giggs]] will take temporary charge of the team until a permanent successor is appointed.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/27107795 |work=BBC Sport |title=David Moyes: Manchester United manager sacked by club |publisher=BBC |date=22 April 2014 |accessdate=22 April 2014}}</ref>
*24 April – [[Chief Secretary to the Treasury]] [[Danny Alexander]] announces that [[Cornish people]] will be granted minority status under [[Council of Europe]] rules governing the protection of national minorities.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-cornwall-27132035 |work=BBC News |title=Cornish people granted minority status within UK |publisher=BBC |date=24 April 2014 |accessdate=25 April 2014}}</ref>
*25 April – The government has launched an inquiry after the ''[[Liverpool Echo]]'' reported that [[Whitehall]] computers were used to post offensive remarks about the [[Hillsborough disaster]] on [[Wikipedia]] pages relating to the topic.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-merseyside-27165844 |work=BBC News |title=Hillsborough Wikipedia posts were 'sickening', Cabinet Office says |publisher=BBC |date=25 April 2014 |accessdate=25 April 2014}}</ref>
*28 April – 
**A youth is detained by police after a female teacher is stabbed to death in front of students at a school in Leeds.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-27193638 |work=BBC News |title=Teacher stabbed to death at Leeds school |publisher=BBC |date=28 April 2014 |accessdate=28 April 2014}}</ref>
**Following a trial at Southwark Crown Court, publicist [[Max Clifford]] is convicted of eight indecent assaults on women and girls.<ref>{{cite news|author=Richard Lister |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-27192600 |work=BBC News |title=Max Clifford guilty of eight indecent assaults |publisher=BBC |date=28 April 2014 |accessdate=28 April 2014}}</ref>
*29 April – Former Conservative MP [[Patrick Mercer]] has resigned from his [[Newark (UK Parliament constituency)|Newark]] seat after he was suspended from Parliament for six months for allegedly asking questions in the House of Commons in exchange for money.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-27208966 |work=BBC News |title=Former Tory MP Mercer resigns after Commons suspension |publisher=BBC |date=29 April 2014 |accessdate=29 April 2014}}</ref>

===May===
*1 May – Barrister and part-time judge [[Constance Briscoe]] is convicted of perverting the course of justice after a trial at the [[Old Bailey]] heard she lied to police investigating the case of former MP [[Chris Huhne]]'s speeding points.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-27239153 |work=BBC News |title=Constance Briscoe faces fresh investigation into fraud |publisher=BBC |date=1 May 2014 |accessdate=2 May 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Coleman |first=Clive |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-27245579 |title=Constance Briscoe's fall from grace |publisher=BBC |work=BBC News |date=1 May 2014 |accessdate=2 May 2014}}</ref> She is sentenced to 16 months in prison the following day.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-27254145 |work=BBC News |title=Constance Briscoe jailed for 16 months for lying to police |publisher=BBC |date=2 May 2014 |accessdate=2 May 2014}}</ref>
*2 May – Publicist Max Clifford is jailed for eight years.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-27259318 |work=BBC News |title=Max Clifford jailed for eight years for sex assaults |publisher=BBC |date=2 May 2014 |accessdate=2 May 2014}}</ref>
*8 May – Defence Secretary [[Philip Hammond]] announces a review of the [[British Armed Forces]] will be brought forward from 2018. The review could allow women to serve in front line combat roles for the first time.<ref>{{cite news|author=Caroline Wyatt |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-27331365 |work=BBC News |title=Army to review female close combat roles |publisher=BBC |date=8 May 2014 |accessdate=8 May 2014}}</ref>
*9 May – The [[2014 Giro d'Italia|Giro d'Italia]] cycle race starts in [[Belfast]].<ref>{{cite web|author=William Fotheringham |url=https://www.theguardian.com/sport/2014/may/03/giro-ditalia-road-belfast-missing-turn-us |title=Giro d'Italia takes road to Belfast after missing turn for US |publisher=Theguardian.com |date= |accessdate=2014-05-04}}</ref>
*21 May – [[Clarence House]] has refused to comment on claims that [[Charles, Prince of Wales|Prince Charles]] compared Russian President [[Vladimir Putin]]'s stance over Ukraine to the actions of [[Adolf Hitler]] before [[World War II]] during a visit to Canada.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-us-canada-27497899 |work=BBC News |title=Prince Charles 'compared Russian actions to Nazis' |publisher=BBC |date=21 May 2014 |accessdate=24 May 2014}}</ref>
