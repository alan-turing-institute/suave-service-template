Events from the year '''2016 in the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland'''. The major political events of this year were dominated by the [[United Kingdom European Union membership referendum, 2016|European Union membership referendum]] and [[Aftermath of the United Kingdom European Union membership referendum, 2016|its aftermath]] (colloquially referred to as "[[Brexit]]").
{{TOC limit|3}}

==Incumbents==
*[[Monarch of the United Kingdom|Monarch]] – [[Elizabeth II]]
*[[Prime Minister of the United Kingdom|Prime Minister]] – [[David Cameron]] ([[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative]]) (until 13 July), [[Theresa May]] ([[Conservative Party (UK)|Conservative]]) (starting 13 July)

== Events ==

=== January ===
*2 January – [[rail transport in Great Britain|Rail fares]] in England, Scotland and Wales rise by 1.1%, in line with current [[Consumer Price Index (United Kingdom)|inflation rates]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-35209136|title=Rail fares increase by 1.1% in England, Scotland and Wales|publisher=BBC|date=2 January 2016|accessdate=4 January 2016}}</ref>
*3 January – [[Islamic State of Iraq and the Levant|Islamic State]] militants release a video that depicts the killing of five men who it says were spying for Britain, and also threatened attacks in the UK.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-35218086|work=BBC News|title=Islamic State video 'shows killing of five men it says spied for UK'|publisher=BBC |date=3 January 2016 |accessdate=4 January 2016}}</ref> The main figure in the video was subsequently believed to be [[Siddhartha Dhar]], a British radical Islamist.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-35228558|work=BBC News|title=Islamic State video suspect thought to be Briton Siddhartha Dhar|publisher=BBC|date=4 January 2016 |accessdate=4 January 2016}}</ref>
*4 January –
**As strong winds and heavy rain continue to batter parts of Scotland, with more than 30 flood warnings issued by [[Scottish Environment Protection Agency|SEPA]], the owner of historic [[Abergeldie Castle]] is forced to evacuate the 16th century tower house in [[Aberdeenshire]] after the [[River Dee, Aberdeenshire|River Dee]] sweeps away land behind the property, leaving it only a few feet from the water.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/35219769 |work=BBC News |title=Drone shows historic castle teetering over flood water |publisher=BBC |date=4 January 2016 |accessdate=4 January 2016}}</ref>
** It is announced that strikes for three days in January and February by [[National Health Service (England)|junior doctors in England]] are back on after talks with the government break down.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/health-35225918 |work=BBC News |title=Junior doctor strikes back on as talks fail |publisher=BBC |date=4 January 2016 |accessdate=4 January 2016}}</ref>
*6 January – [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour]] MPs [[Jonathan Reynolds]] and [[Stephen Doughty]] quit over the sacking of the shadow Europe minister [[Pat McFadden (British politician)|Pat McFadden]], after party leader [[Jeremy Corbyn]] reshuffles his shadow cabinet and makes controversial changes within his team.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-35239232|title=Labour reshuffle: Shadow ministers quit in protest|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=6 January 2016|accessdate=6 January 2016}}</ref>
*9 January – 
**Arthur Simpson-Kent, the partner of former ''[[EastEnders]]'' actress [[Sian Blake]] who was murdered alongside her two sons in December, is arrested in [[Ghana]] on suspicion of her murder.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-35273005|title=Sian Blake: Partner held in Ghana over EastEnders actress's murder|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=9 January 2016|accessdate=10 January 2016}}</ref>
**Scotland's [[First Minister of Scotland|First Minister]], [[Nicola Sturgeon]], announces that every household, business or charity in the country directly affected by flood water will get a grant of £1,500.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-35269135 |title=£1,500 grants for homes and businesses hit by floods|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=9 January 2016|accessdate=9 January 2016}}</ref>
*11 January
**In the aftermath of Jeremy Corbyn's reshuffle of the Labour Party, [[Attorney General|Shadow Attorney General]] [[Catherine McKinnell]] resigns, citing party infighting, family reasons and the desire to speak in Parliament beyond her legal portfolio.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-35281203?ns_mchannel=social&ns_campaign=bbc_breaking&ns_source=twitter&ns_linkname=news_central |title=Labour's Catherine McKinnell quits shadow cabinet |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=11 January 2016 |accessdate=2 February 2016}}</ref>
**[[Arlene Foster]] becomes the first women to lead the [[Democratic Unionist Party]] and becomes [[Northern Ireland]] First Women [[First Minister of Northern Ireland|First Minister]].<ref>{{cite news|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-35127740}}</ref>
*12 January – Junior doctors in England providing non-emergency care strike for 24 hours in a dispute with Health Secretary [[Jeremy Hunt]] over pay and working hours.<ref>{{cite web|title=Junior doctors' strike: increasing optimism over deal to end dispute|url=https://www.theguardian.com/society/2016/jan/12/junior-doctors-strike-all-sides-increasingly-optimistic-deal-end-dispute|publisher=The Guardian|accessdate=29 January 2016}}</ref>
*13 January – MPs give initial support to the idea of [[National anthem of England|England adopting an official national anthem]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-derbyshire-35296296 |title=MPs back calls for English national anthem|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=13 January 2016|accessdate=13 January 2016}}</ref>
*14 January – 
**The gang of "brazen burglars" involved in the £14m [[Hatton Garden safe deposit burglary|Hatton Garden jewellery heist]], dubbed the "largest burglary in English legal history", face jail after the final three are convicted of involvement.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-35315472|title=Hatton Garden jewellery heist: Final three guilty over £14m burglary|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=14 January 2016|accessdate=14 January 2016}}</ref>
**The [[Metropolitan Police]] announce that an extra 600 armed officers are to be trained and patrols more than doubled to help counter the threat of a terrorist attack in London.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-35308467|title=Met Police to get 600 more armed police to boost terror response|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=14 January 2016|accessdate=14 January 2016}}</ref>
*15 January – [[Tim Peake]] conducts the first ever [[spacewalk]] by an "official" British astronaut, stepping outside an [[International Space Station|ISS]] airlock just after 13:00 GMT.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/science-environment-35273912|title=Tim Peake begins historic spacewalk|work=BBC News|date=15 January 2016|accessdate=15 January 2016}}</ref>
*17 January – For the first time, the typical pay of a Scottish worker rises above those in England, according to a new study by the [[Resolution Foundation]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-scotland-35337288|title=Typical Scottish worker's pay tops England for first time|work=BBC News |publisher=BBC|date=17 January 2016|accessdate=17 January 2016}}</ref>
*20 January – [[Unemployment in the United Kingdom|Unemployment rates]] fall to 5.1%, their lowest level in almost a decade, but figures show that wage growth has slowed.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-35359689|title=UK jobless rate at 10-year low but wage growth slows|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=20 January 2016|accessdate=20 January 2016}}</ref>
*21 January – 
**An inquiry finds that the murder of British ex-Russian spy [[Alexander Litvinenko]] in 2006 in London was "probably" approved by [[Russian President]] [[Vladimir Putin]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-35370819|title=President Putin 'probably' approved Litvinenko murder|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=21 January 2016|accessdate=21 January 2016}}</ref>
**Figures show that murders and killings in England and Wales have increased to their highest level for five years, largely due to an abnormally high number of deaths in June when 75 people were killed.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-35372940|title=Murder rate rises after spate of killings in June 2015|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=21 January 2016|accessdate=21 January 2016}}</ref>
*28 January – After three weeks of appeals, [[Camelot Group|Camelot]] receive a "valid claim" for the record breaking £33m [[National Lottery (United Kingdom)|Lotto]] jackpot prize drawn on 9 January.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-35432234|title=£33m Lotto jackpot winner to remain anonymous|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=28 January 2016|accessdate=28 January 2016}}</ref>
*29 January – The last [[Land Rover Defender]] rolls off the production line at [[Solihull plant|Solihull]], ending 68 years of production.<ref>{{cite news|title=Land Rover Defender Solihull production ends|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-35436741|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=29 January 2016|accessdate=29 January 2016}}</ref>

*1 February – Scientists are given the go-ahead by regulators to genetically modify [[human embryos]] which were to be destroyed in seven days.<ref>{{cite news|title=Scientists get 'gene editing' go-ahead|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/health-35459054|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=1 February 2016|accessdate=1 February 2016}}</ref>

*3 February – The [[High Court of England and Wales|High Court]] gives permission for [[John Bingham, 7th Earl of Lucan|Lord Lucan]] to be declared dead, and for a [[death certificate]] to be issued 42 years after his disappearance.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-35481376 |title=Lord Lucan death certificate granted |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=3 February 2016 |accessdate=3 February 2016}}</ref>
*6 February – Britain's most famous steam locomotive, the ''[[LNER Class A3 4472 Flying Scotsman|Flying Scotsman]]'', returns to public service after a 10-year restoration project.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-35515743|title=Famous Flying Scotsman goes full steam ahead|work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=7 February 2016 |accessdate=7 February 2016}}</ref>
*8 February – [[Storm Imogen]] hits Britain, causing thousands of power outages and structural damage across the country, along with disruption for many commuters.<ref>{{cite news|last=Finnigan |first=Lexi |url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/weather/12145886/UK-weather-Storm-Imogen-lashes-Britain-with-gales-and-torrential-rain-live.html |title=Storm Imogen: Record breaking winds amid news an RSPCA worker may have been swept out to sea |publisher=Telegraph Media Group |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |date=8 February 2016 |accessdate=12 February 2016}}</ref>
*10 February – Junior doctors walk out in their second recent strike over Saturday working arrangements, causing disruption to medical services.<ref>{{cite news|last=Hindocha |first=Sumeet |url=https://www.theguardian.com/society/live/2016/feb/10/junior-doctors-strike-second-24-hour-walkout-over-new-contracts-live-updates |title=Junior doctors strike: Hunt claims 43% of doctors working |publisher=Guardian Media Group |newspaper=The Guardian |date=10 February 2016 |accessdate=12 February 2016}}</ref>
*12 February – 
**After many years as print newspapers, it is announced that the UK newspapers ''[[The Independent]]'' and the ''[[Independent on Sunday]]'' will cease to print and become online-only at the end of March.<ref>{{cite news|first=Mark |last=Sweney |url=https://www.theguardian.com/media/2016/feb/12/independent-and-independent-on-sunday-closures-confirmed |title=Independent and Independent on Sunday print closures confirmed |publisher=Guardian Media Group |newspaper=The Guardian |date=12 February 2016 |accessdate=12 February 2016}}</ref> Its stablemate, the ''[[i (newspaper)|i]]'', will be sold to [[Johnston Press]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-35561145 |title=Independent to cease as print edition |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=12 February 2016 |accessdate=12 February 2016}}</ref>
**Work begins to join the [[Great Central Railway (heritage railway)|Leicestershire]] and [[Great Central Railway (Nottingham)|Nottinghamshire]] sections of the preserved Great Central Railway, by filling in the [[Loughborough Gap]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-leicestershire-35534096 |title=Mind the Gap: Great Central Railway to be joined after 40 years |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=12 February 2016 |accessdate=12 February 2016}}</ref>
**A temporary footbridge is opened over the [[River Wharfe]] at [[Tadcaster]], North Yorkshire, reuniting the town following the damage done to the original [[Tadcaster Bridge|bridge]] in the December 2015 floods.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-york-north-yorkshire-35557412 |title=Tadcaster footbridge over Wharfe opens to reunite flood-split town |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=12 February 2016 |accessdate=12 February 2016}}</ref>
*13 February – All four members of British [[indie pop]] group, [[Viola Beach]], along with the band's manager, are killed in a car crash in Sweden.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-35572838 |title=UK band Viola Beach 'killed in Swedish car crash' |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=14 February 2016 |accessdate=14 February 2016}}</ref>
*15 February – A [[Virgin Atlantic]] flight heading for [[New York City]] turns back to [[London Heathrow Airport]] after a laser beam is shone into the plane's cockpit and injures one of the co-pilots.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-35575861|title=Virgin Atlantic flight back in UK after 'laser incident'|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=15 February 2016|accessdate=16 February 2016}}</ref>
*16 February – [[BBC Three]] becomes the first UK television network to become online only, having broadcast for its final night after 13 years as a television channel.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-35578867|title=BBC Three moves online after final night as TV channel|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=16 February 2016|accessdate=16 February 2016}}</ref>
*19 February – [[Royal Mail]] announce that the price of first class stamps will increase by one penny.<ref>{{cite news|first=Rob |last=Davies |url=https://www.theguardian.com/business/2016/feb/19/royal-mail-raises-stamp-prices |title=Royal Mail raises stamp prices by 1p |publisher=Guardian Media Group |newspaper=The Guardian |date=19 February 2016 |accessdate=19 March 2016}}</ref>
*20 February – [[David Cameron]] announces that Britain will hold a [[United Kingdom European Union membership referendum, 2016|referendum]] on the United Kingdom's membership of the [[European Union]] on 23 June.<ref name="autogenerated1">{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-35621079|title=EU referendum: Cameron announces date of vote|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=20 February 2016|accessdate=20 February 2016}}</ref>
*21 February – [[Mayor of London]] [[Boris Johnson]] controversially announces he is to defy David Cameron, and campaign for the UK to leave the European Union.<ref>{{cite news|title=EU referendum: Time to vote for real change, says Boris Johnson|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-eu-referendum-35626621|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=22 February 2016|accessdate=23 February 2016}}</ref>
*22 February – The [[Pound sterling|pound]] hits its lowest level against the [[United States dollar|dollar]], falling down as much as 2.4%, in almost seven years amid concerns about a possible [[United Kingdom European Union membership referendum, 2016|exit from the European Union]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/business-35628733|title=Pound hits lowest level against dollar since 2009|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=22 February 2016|accessdate=22 February 2016}}</ref>
*23 February –
**A major incident is declared as one person dies, and three are left missing after an explosion at [[Didcot Power Station]] in Oxfordshire.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-oxfordshire-35641766|title=Casualties feared after Didcot Power Station 'explosion'|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=23 February 2016|accessdate=23 February 2016}}</ref>
**Ebola nurse Pauline Cafferkey is admitted to hospital again for the third time after first contracting the virus in [[Sierra Leone]] in 2014.<ref>{{cite news|first=Haroon|last=Siddique|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2016/feb/23/ebola-nurse-pauline-cafferkey-returns-to-hospital|title=Ebola nurse Pauline Cafferkey transferred to Royal Free hospital|publisher=Guardian Media Group|newspaper=The Guardian|date=23 February 2016|accessdate=23 February 2016}}</ref>
**It is announced that the new [[Crossrail]] line in London will be called the "Elizabeth Line", after Queen [[Elizabeth II]].<ref>{{cite news|title=London's Crossrail to be called the Elizabeth Line|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-london-35640494|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=23 February 2016|accessdate=23 February 2016}}</ref>
*26 February – 
**Six [[Rotherham]] individuals, including three brothers and their uncle, who [[Rotherham child sexual exploitation scandal#Subsequent arrests and trials|systematically groomed, sexually assaulted and raped]] fifteen teenage girls are sentenced to a combined 103 years in prison.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-35670538|title=Hussain brothers jailed in Rotherham abuse case|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=26 February 2016|accessdate=26 February 2016}}</ref>
**Three British tourists fall to their deaths after being taken to the Datanla waterfalls by an unlicensed tour guide in [[Da Lat]], [[Vietnam]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-35670529|title=Britons die near Datanla waterfalls in Vietnam|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|date=26 February 2016|accessdate=26 February 2016}}</ref>

*21 March – 
**Clayton Williams, the car thief who ran over and killed PC Dave Phillips in [[Merseyside]] in October 2015, is found guilty of manslaughter and sentenced to 20 years in jail.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-35860607|title=Clayton Williams guilty of PC Dave Phillips' manslaughter|work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=21 March 2016 |accessdate=21 March 2016}}</ref>
**Brian Reader, the ringleader in the [[Hatton Garden safe deposit burglary|Hatton Garden jewellery heist]], and the last of the gang to be sentenced, is given more than six years in jail.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-35865512|title=Hatton Garden heist ringleader jailed|work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=21 March 2016 |accessdate=21 March 2016}}</ref>

*23 March – Drivers on [[London Underground]]'s [[Piccadilly line]] go on strike for 24 hours over "bullying".<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-london-35871063| |title=Piccadilly Line tube drivers' strike action starts |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=24 March 2016 |accessdate=24 March 2016}}</ref>
